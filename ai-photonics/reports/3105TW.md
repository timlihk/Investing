# 3105.TW - WIN Semiconductors
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 64/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
WIN Semiconductors is the world's largest pure-play GaAs/GaN/SiC foundry with 50%+ GaAs foundry market share, providing the compound semiconductor fabrication services used to make VCSELs, power amplifiers, and optical components for data center and AI applications. While traditionally focused on smartphones (RF front-end), WIN is pivoting to AI/satellite/optical applications with more-loyal customer relationships. The ASE acquisition of WIN's Kaohsiung plant for advanced packaging highlights the strategic value of compound semiconductor capacity in the AI era.

### Moat: Strong (3.5/5)
- 50%+ GaAs foundry share is dominant in compound semiconductors.
- GaAs VCSELs are used in 3D sensing AND data center short-reach optical interconnects.
- Pivot to AI/satellite diversifies away from cyclical mobile RF business.
- ASE plant sale validates strategic value of compound semi facilities.

### Supply Chain Position
- **Upstream foundry for**: VCSEL makers (Lumentum, Coherent), RF chip designers, optical component companies
- **GaAs VCSELs → data center**: Short-reach optical interconnects use GaAs VCSEL arrays
- **Competes with**: IQE (epiwafers), Skyworks (captive GaAs fab), AWSC

### Verdict: HOLD
WIN is the GaAs foundry analog of Tower Semiconductor in SiPh -- the dominant compound semiconductor foundry with 50%+ share. The pivot to AI/optical is promising but revenue is still heavily mobile-dependent. More interesting than IQE (which only does epiwafers) because WIN is a full foundry. Watch for AI/optical revenue to exceed 30% of total.
