# CLS - Celestica Inc
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 74/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Celestica is the "bigger Fabrinet" of AI infrastructure -- a $12.4B revenue electronics manufacturing services (EMS) company that builds custom Ethernet switches, AI networking hardware, and data center infrastructure for hyperscalers, with 55% market share in custom Ethernet switch manufacturing. Revenue growing 28% to FY2025 and guided to $17B in FY2026 (+37%), with the Connectivity & Cloud Solutions (CCS) segment representing 75% of revenue and growing 40%. This is one of the most direct, least appreciated "picks and shovels" plays on AI data center construction.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Strong (3.5/5)**

- **55% custom Ethernet switch manufacturing share**: When hyperscalers design custom switches (rather than buying Arista), they manufacture at Celestica. This is the hardware-level equivalent of Fabrinet's optical assembly dominance.
- **CCS platform stickiness**: Once a hyperscaler qualifies Celestica for switch/server/networking manufacturing, switching costs are high (re-qualification takes 12-18 months).
- **Scale advantage**: At $12.4B revenue, Celestica has the manufacturing scale that smaller EMS companies can't match for high-complexity AI networking hardware.
- **AI server expertise**: Manufactures AI-optimized server platforms with GPU/accelerator integration.
- **Risk**: EMS margins are structurally low (single-digit %). Customer concentration with top hyperscalers.

### Supply Chain Position
Celestica sits between chip designers and end-users:
- **Buys from**: Broadcom (switching ASICs), Credo (retimers/AEC), MACOM (analog ICs), Innolight/Coherent (transceivers)
- **Sells to**: Hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN), OEMs building AI data centers
- **Competes with**: Fabrinet (FN) in optical assembly, Flex/Jabil in broader EMS

### Catalysts
1. **FY26 revenue raised to $17B**: Strong demand signal from hyperscaler AI capex.
2. **CCS +40% growth**: AI networking and compute driving the fastest-growing segment.
3. **Custom switch market expansion**: As hyperscalers increasingly design custom switches (vs buying Arista/Cisco), Celestica captures the manufacturing.
4. **$1B capex investment**: Building manufacturing capacity for next-gen AI infrastructure.
5. **Market share expansion**: 55% custom Ethernet switch share provides a strong base for growth.

### Risks
1. **Low EMS margins**: Manufacturing services inherently operate at single-digit margins. Any cost overrun compresses profitability.
2. **Customer concentration**: Top hyperscaler customers represent majority of revenue.
3. **Cyclical AI capex**: If hyperscaler spending pauses, Celestica's revenue growth decelerates sharply.
4. **Competition from Flex/Jabil**: Larger EMS companies could pursue the same custom AI networking manufacturing market.
5. **Commodity perception**: Market may view Celestica as a low-margin contract manufacturer rather than an AI infrastructure play, capping valuation.

### Verdict: HOLD (near BUY)
Celestica is a compelling under-appreciated AI infrastructure play with 37% revenue growth guidance, 55% custom Ethernet switch share, and direct exposure to every dollar of AI data center capex. The FY26 guide raise to $17B from $16B signals management confidence. At ~$12B market cap on $17B FY26 revenue (~0.7x sales), the stock is extremely cheap if you believe the AI buildout continues. Would upgrade to BUY on confirmation that CCS margins are expanding, or on any pullback that brings the stock to a level where the risk/reward is more asymmetric.
