### Business Segments
Delta operates three segments, all benefiting from AI data center buildout:

1. **Power Electronics (~45-50% of revenue)**: Server power supplies, EV charging, telecom power. GB300 shipments drove Q4 2025 growth. Delta is a top-3 global server power supply vendor.
2. **Infrastructure (~30-35% of revenue)**: Data center infrastructure including liquid cooling, BBU/UPS, and the AGEMA networking/optical products. This segment showed strongest YoY and QoQ growth in 2025, driven by data center demand.
3. **Automation (~15-20% of revenue)**: Industrial automation, building automation. Slowest growing segment.

### Optical / Photonics Exposure Assessment

**AGEMA Networking Switches**: Delta's AGEMA line offers open networking switches from 1G to 400G based on Broadcom ASICs. At OFC'26, Delta demonstrated the DC-9064O, a 102.4T Ethernet switch for the 1.6TbE generation using 224G/lane signaling. This positions Delta as a competitor to Edgecore (Accton), Celestica, and other white box switch ODMs -- but AGEMA's revenue contribution appears to be a low single-digit percentage of total Infrastructure segment revenue.

**Optical Transceivers**: Delta's AGEMA product page lists optical transceivers (400G), but Delta is NOT a major transceiver manufacturer. At OFC'26, Delta partnered with Source Photonics to demo 1.6T transceivers -- Source Photonics provided the optics while Delta provided the switch platform. Notably, Source Photonics was previously a Delta subsidiary but was acquired by Suzhou Dongshan Precision Manufacturing (DSBJ) of China. Delta no longer owns an optical transceiver business.

**Liquid Cooling & Power**: The real AI data center story at Delta is liquid cooling solutions and high-efficiency server power supplies for GPU clusters. These are growing rapidly but are NOT photonics.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Weak for Photonics (1.5/5)**

Delta's moat in power supplies and thermal management is strong (top-3 global position, long customer relationships, scale manufacturing). But in the photonics value chain specifically:
- **AGEMA switches**: Commodity white box switches using Broadcom merchant silicon. No differentiation vs. Edgecore, Celestica, or other ODMs. Low switching costs.
- **Optical transceivers**: Delta does not manufacture transceivers at scale. The Source Photonics partnership is a demo, not a production program.
- **No proprietary optical IP**: Unlike Coherent, Lumentum, or MACOM, Delta has no laser, modulator, or optical IC IP.

### Catalysts
1. **AI data center power/cooling demand**: Server power supply and liquid cooling revenue growing 40%+ YoY. This is the real Delta bull case.
2. **AGEMA 1.6T switch ramp**: If the DC-9064O wins hyperscaler design slots, it could grow the networking business -- but competition is fierce.
3. **Infrastructure segment margin expansion**: Mix shift toward higher-margin data center products could lift operating margins above 20%.
4. **Forward P/E compression**: If FY26 delivers TWD 53+ EPS, the 32x forward P/E looks more reasonable for a 30%+ grower.

### Risks
1. **Optical exposure is immaterial**: Buying Delta for photonics is like buying GE for jet engine blades. The exposure exists but is dwarfed by other segments.
2. **75x trailing P/E**: Priced for perfection. Any deceleration in AI capex would compress the multiple severely.
3. **Conglomerate discount risk**: Automation and telecom segments may drag on the AI re-rating narrative.
4. **Chinese competition**: In power supplies and white box switches, Chinese competitors offer lower-cost alternatives.
5. **Source Photonics is gone**: Delta sold its optical transceiver subsidiary. Re-entry into transceivers from scratch is unlikely.

### Valuation
At TWD 4.5T market cap and TWD 555B revenue, Delta trades at 8.1x TTM sales -- elevated for an industrial conglomerate but below semiconductor multiples. The 75x trailing P/E reflects the massive 2025 earnings step-up from AI demand. If FY26 consensus of TWD 53+ EPS is achieved, the forward P/E of 32x is fair for a 30%+ growth industrial. However, this is an AI infrastructure play on power and cooling, not a photonics investment. A target price of TWD 1,700 (roughly in line with analyst median) reflects the power/cooling growth story but assigns no premium for optical exposure.

### Verdict: AVOID (for photonics portfolio)
Delta Electronics is a legitimate AI data center infrastructure winner -- but through power supplies and liquid cooling, not optical transceivers or photonics. The AGEMA networking switch line is real but tiny relative to the company, and Delta no longer owns an optical transceiver business (Source Photonics was sold). For a photonics-focused portfolio, the optical exposure is immaterial and diluted by ~98% non-optical revenue. Investors seeking AI infrastructure exposure through Delta should understand they are buying power/cooling with a networking sidecar, not a photonics chokepoint.
