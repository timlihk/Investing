# VNP.TO - 5N Plus
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 61/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
5N Plus is a Canadian specialty materials company that refines and produces ultra-high-purity indium, gallium, germanium, and tellurium -- the critical raw materials used in compound semiconductor wafers (InP, GaAs, Ge) that underpin all photonic chips and optical transceivers. Revenue surging 35% to $391M in FY2025 with Q4 revenue beating estimates by 10%+, and a $18.1M US government grant for germanium capacity expansion. While primarily solar-focused (CdTe), the semiconductor materials division directly supplies the photonics supply chain. At C$2.77B market cap, this is the deepest upstream play in the AI photonics value chain.

### Moat: Moderate (2.5/5)
- Ultra-high-purity metals refining requires specialized know-how and EPA-compliant facilities.
- Supplies AXT, IQE, and other epiwafer makers with refined indium, gallium, germanium.
- US government grant for germanium signals strategic importance for domestic semiconductor supply chain.
- But commodity-adjacent -- raw materials are less differentiated than processed wafers or chips.

### Supply Chain Position
**The deepest upstream layer**:
```
5N Plus (raw metals) → AXT/IQE (epiwafers) → Tower Semi/GF (SiPh foundry) → Photonic ICs → Transceivers → AI data centers
```
Every InP wafer starts with purified indium and phosphorus. Every GaAs VCSEL starts with purified gallium and arsenic. 5N Plus refines these metals to semiconductor-grade purity.

### Verdict: HOLD
5N Plus is the most upstream name in the entire AI photonics supply chain -- supplying the raw metals that become compound semiconductor wafers. Revenue +35% and +322% stock return show the market is beginning to recognize this positioning. However, the company is primarily solar-focused (CdTe) with semiconductor as a secondary driver, and raw materials are less differentiated than processed components. Interesting for deep supply chain exposure but not a primary photonics play.
