# 300408.SZ - Chaozhou Three-Circle (Group) Co., Ltd.
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Score: 62/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Chaozhou Three-Circle is the world's largest ceramic ferrule manufacturer by volume, producing over 700 million ferrules annually and commanding >28% of global output -- a genuine chokepoint in the AI photonics supply chain where every fiber optic connector requires a sub-micron-tolerance zirconia ceramic ferrule. With 42% gross margins, 25% revenue growth, a CNY 9B (~$1.25B) revenue base diversified across MLCCs and ceramic substrates, and a new 400M-unit Jiangxi capacity expansion completed in 2024, Three-Circle is the low-cost, high-volume toll collector on the explosive growth of AI optical interconnects. However, the ferrule business is only one segment of a diversified electronic components company, and the stock trades at 43x trailing earnings after a significant rally.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate-Strong (3.5/5)**

Three-Circle's moat is scale manufacturing + cost leadership + precision ceramics expertise:
- **#1 global volume producer**: >28% of global ferrule output (700M+ ferrules/year) across SC, LC, and MPO types. This is the largest single-source in the world by volume.
- **Cost leadership**: Chinese manufacturing cost structure combined with automated production and robotic polishing gives Three-Circle a structural cost advantage over Japanese competitors (Seikoh Giken, Kyocera, Orbray).
- **Capacity expansion**: New Jiangxi facility (completed March 2024) added 400M annual ferrule capacity with robotic polishing, improving first-pass yield by ~21%.
- **Diversified product portfolio**: MLCCs, ceramic substrates, and ferrules share zirconia ceramic processing know-how, creating R&D and manufacturing synergies.
- **"National manufacturing champion"**: Ceramic substrates and optical fiber ferrules won China's national manufacturing single champion product designation, providing regulatory and procurement advantages in domestic market.
- **Limitations**: Ferrules are more commoditized than Seikoh Giken's combined ferrule + polishing equipment model. Three-Circle competes primarily on volume and cost, not on equipment lock-in.

### Catalysts
1. **AI data center fabric densification**: 20-40x more connectors per rack in AI clusters vs traditional data centers. Each 800G->1.6T->3.2T transition requires new connector generations with more ferrules per port.
2. **MLCC revenue acceleration (+57% YoY in H1 2024)**: MLCCs are used in every electronic device; AI server demand is driving content growth. This diversified growth supports the overall business.
3. **Capacity expansion completion**: Jiangxi facility adding 400M ferrules/year is now operational, positioning Three-Circle to capture share as demand surges.
4. **MPO/MTP transition**: Multi-fiber connectors for 800G+ optics use multiple ferrules per connector, increasing content per port.
5. **Forward P/E compression**: At 24x forward P/E (vs 43x trailing), if growth sustains, the valuation becomes more reasonable.

### Risks
1. **Segment opacity**: Three-Circle does not clearly break out ferrule revenue from total optical communication components. The ferrule business may be a smaller portion than the headline suggests.
2. **Valuation after rally**: Stock has nearly doubled from 52-week low of CNY 31.30. At 43x trailing P/E, significant growth is priced in.
3. **Commoditization pressure**: Unlike Seikoh Giken (which sells polishing equipment + ferrules in a razor/blade model), Three-Circle competes primarily on volume and cost. This is a less defensible position.
4. **Chinese market concentration**: ~90% of global ferrule production is in China. Geopolitical risk if Western customers seek supply chain diversification.
5. **MLCC competition**: The MLCC market is dominated by Murata, Samsung Electro-Mechanics, and TDK. Three-Circle is a smaller player in a brutally competitive segment.
6. **Geopolitical risk**: Shenzhen-listed Chinese company -- subject to US-China tensions, potential sanctions risk, and VIE structure concerns for foreign investors.

### Valuation
At 43x trailing P/E and 12.7x P/S, Three-Circle is priced as a premium growth company. 2024 net income was CNY 2.62B on revenue of CNY 9.0B. If revenue grows 20% to CNY 10.8B in 2025 with maintained 29% profit margins, net income could reach CNY 3.1B, giving a forward P/E of ~37x on current market cap. The forward P/E of 24x (per Yahoo Finance) implies consensus estimates of CNY 4.7B net income, which would require ~80% earnings growth -- ambitious but plausible if MLCC and ferrule demand both accelerate. Compared to Seikoh Giken at 71x trailing P/E, Three-Circle offers better value for a similar ferrule exposure, though with lower photonics purity and a less defensible moat. Fair value estimate: CNY 45-50 based on 30x forward P/E on estimated 2025 earnings of CNY 3.1B.

### Verdict: HOLD
Chaozhou Three-Circle is a genuine, high-quality participant in the AI photonics supply chain as the world's largest ceramic ferrule manufacturer by volume. The 42% gross margins, 25% revenue growth, and near-zero debt make it a financially strong business. However, it is not as pure a photonics chokepoint as Seikoh Giken -- the ferrule business is only ~36% of revenue, the competitive moat is based on cost/volume rather than equipment lock-in, and the stock has already doubled. At 43x trailing P/E with geopolitical risk, the risk/reward is unfavorable for new positions. Monitor for a pullback to CNY 40-45 (25-30% downside) for a better entry, or consider Seikoh Giken for purer ferrule exposure despite its even more stretched valuation.
