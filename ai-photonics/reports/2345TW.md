### Business Overview

**Accton Technology** is Taiwan's leading networking ODM, operating through two key channels:

1. **ODM/OEM for Hyperscalers (~75-80% of revenue)**: Designs and manufactures networking switches sold directly to hyperscalers (Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon) or through their supply chains. These are "white label" -- sold under the customer's brand or as bare-metal platforms.

2. **Edgecore Networks (~20-25% of revenue)**: Accton's own-brand subsidiary selling open networking switches to enterprises, telcos, and smaller cloud operators. Edgecore is the #1 open networking switch brand globally.

### AI Data Center Relevance

**Meta Partnership**: Accton is the ODM behind Meta's Minipack and Minipack2 switches -- the backbone of Meta's F16 data center network. These are modular 100G/400G switches deployed as fabric, spine, and aggregator. The relationship is deep and sticky.

**102.4T Next-Gen Switches**: In February 2026, Edgecore launched the AIS1600-64O (64 x 1.6T OSFP1600 ports) and AIS800-128O (128 x 800G OSFP800 ports) -- the industry's first commercially available 102.4T open networking switches, built on Broadcom's 3nm Tomahawk 6 silicon.

**OCP Contributions**: Accton contributed AI/ML-optimized DDC (Distributed Disaggregated Chassis) 800G designs to OCP, cementing its position as the reference ODM for hyperscale open networking.

**Optical Wavelength Switch (OWS)**: Edgecore is developing OWS technology that switches signals at the optical layer, bypassing electrical conversion. This is an early-stage photonics play within Accton's portfolio.

**TensorWave AI Partnership**: As of April 2026, Edgecore is partnering with TensorWave to showcase 102.4T networking for AMD-based AI infrastructure, demonstrating reach beyond NVIDIA-centric deployments.

### Photonics Nexus: Every Port Consumes Optics

The critical link to our photonics thesis: **every switch port in an Edgecore/Accton switch consumes an optical transceiver**. A single AIS1600-64O switch with 64 x 1.6T ports needs 64 optical transceivers (from Innolight, Coherent, Lumentum, etc.). As Accton ships more 800G and 1.6T switches, it directly drives demand for optical transceivers from our portfolio companies. Accton is a demand indicator, not a supply chokepoint.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate-Strong (3/5)**

- **ODM design expertise**: Deep co-development relationships with hyperscalers (Meta Minipack, Google). Switching costs are moderate -- hyperscalers could in theory use Celestica or others, but requalification takes 12-18 months.
- **First-mover in 102.4T**: Being first to market with TH6-based switches builds reference design advantage.
- **Scale manufacturing**: Accton's TWD 248B revenue base gives cost advantages over smaller white box players.
- **OCP ecosystem leadership**: Accton's OCP contributions create soft lock-in for hyperscalers committed to open networking.
- **Limitation -- ODM economics**: 18% gross margins reflect the reality that Broadcom captures the silicon value and hyperscalers have negotiating power. Accton does not own the switching ASIC, the optical components, or the NOS (network operating system). It provides design + manufacturing.

### Catalysts
1. **800G/1.6T upgrade cycle**: The transition to 800G and 1.6T Ethernet is a multi-year switch refresh cycle. Accton's first-mover 102.4T platforms should capture disproportionate share.
2. **AI cluster buildout acceleration**: Every new AI data center from Meta, Google, Microsoft, xAI, etc. needs switches. Accton's FY25 revenue more than doubled -- and the cycle continues.
3. **Enterprise/telco diversification**: Edgecore branded revenue growing to 25% of total would improve margin mix.
4. **OWS (Optical Wavelength Switch)**: If Edgecore commercializes optical switching, it would move Accton from "adjacent to photonics" to "inside photonics."
5. **Forward P/E of 22x**: If FY26 delivers TWD 82+ EPS, the stock is reasonably priced for a 50%+ grower.

### Risks
1. **Customer concentration**: Meta and a handful of hyperscalers represent the vast majority of revenue. Loss of a single customer would be devastating.
2. **ODM margin ceiling**: 18% gross margin may not expand meaningfully. Broadcom and hyperscalers capture most of the value.
3. **Celestica competition**: Celestica (CLS) is the other major white box switch ODM and is gaining share with NVIDIA-ecosystem switches.
4. **Broadcom dependency**: All Accton switches run Broadcom ASICs. If Broadcom favors a different ODM partner or vertically integrates (unlikely), Accton loses its platform.
5. **Geopolitical risk**: Taiwan manufacturing concentration. US-China tensions could disrupt supply chains.
6. **Valuation after 280% run**: The stock has nearly quadrupled from its 52-week low. Much of the growth is priced in.

### Valuation
At TWD 1.03T market cap and TWD 248B FY25 revenue, Accton trades at 4.2x TTM sales -- actually modest for a 125% grower. The 39x trailing P/E compresses to 22x forward on consensus TWD 82 EPS. If FY26 delivers ~TWD 350B revenue (42% growth) with 13% operating margins, operating income would be ~TWD 45B, net income ~TWD 36B, and EPS ~TWD 64-65 on a diluted basis (conservative). At 25x P/E on TWD 65 EPS, fair value is TWD 1,625 -- roughly current levels. Upside requires either faster growth or margin expansion. Analyst high target of TWD 2,400 implies continued 50%+ growth with margin improvement. At TWD 1,835, the stock is fairly valued for the base case with upside optionality.

### Verdict: HOLD
Accton/Edgecore is a genuine AI networking winner and the best pure-play on open networking switch demand for AI data centers. The 125% FY25 revenue growth, first-to-market 102.4T switches, and Meta ODM relationship are all real competitive advantages. However, for our photonics portfolio, Accton is an adjacent enabler rather than a chokepoint -- it consumes optical transceivers but does not manufacture them. The 18% gross margin reflects ODM economics, not chokepoint pricing power. The stock is fairly priced at 22x forward P/E. Useful as a demand indicator for the optical transceiver supply chain (Innolight, Coherent, Lumentum, AAOI).
