# TEL - TE Connectivity plc
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Score: 66/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
TE Connectivity is the world's largest connector company by revenue ($17.3B FY2025, $18.1B TTM) and the second half of the Amphenol/TE duopoly in AI data center interconnects, but with significantly less AI concentration -- data center/AI is ~15% of total revenue vs Amphenol's ~37%+ IT datacom mix. The Digital Data Networks (DDN) business grew 70% YoY to $707M in Q1 FY2026, and management raised the FY2026 AI revenue outlook by $200M, targeting over $1B in AI revenue this fiscal year with a trajectory toward $3B+. The RAM Photonics acquisition and OFC 2026 CPO/CPC demonstrations show TE is building genuine optical/photonic capabilities, but the stock's heavy automotive/industrial weighting (Transportation ~60% of revenue) dilutes the AI thesis relative to Amphenol.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Strong (3.5/5)**
- Part of the Amphenol/TE/Molex connector triopoly, but TE and Amphenol are the clear top 2 with combined ~50%+ share in high-speed data center connectors.
- TE's DDN business provides high-speed backplane connectors, OSFP connectors and cable assemblies (200G-400G+), power connectors, and thermal management solutions for every major hyperscaler.
- RAM Photonics acquisition adds high-density fiber array units (FAUs) -- critical for co-packaged optics (CPO).
- OFC 2026 demos: CPC-to-CPO architecture with 1.6T LRO + 3.2T CPO integration, CPO-to-optical-backplane systems. TE is building genuine photonic interconnect capabilities.
- AdrenaLINE Catapult ultra-high-density connectors for next-gen AI architectures.
- However, TE's moat is diluted by the massive automotive/industrial business (~60% of revenue). Amphenol is more AI-concentrated.
- 35.7% gross margins are adequate but not exceptional -- this is an industrial connector company, not a semiconductor chokepoint.

### AI Photonics Supply Chain Position
- **Electrical connectors**: High-speed backplane, OSFP, PCIe Gen 6/7, power connectors for AI racks. This is TE's bread and butter in data centers.
- **Optical connectors**: LC fiber optic connectors, fiber assemblies, optical patch panels. Traditional telecom-grade fiber optics.
- **Photonic-specific**: RAM Photonics acquisition (FAUs for CPO), OSFP-XD LPO transceiver (PCIe Gen 6 over optics, 256 GB/s), 1.6T/3.2T CPO socket integration. This is newer and still emerging.
- **Key distinction**: TE is transitioning from purely electrical to hybrid electrical+optical. They're building the physical packaging and connector layer for CPO, not making the photonic chips themselves.

### Supply Chain Position
- **Sells to**: Every hyperscaler (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon), OEMs (Dell, HPE, Cisco), data center builders
- **Complements**: Corning (fiber), Arista (switches), NVIDIA (GPU racks), Broadcom (switch ASICs)
- **Competes with**: Amphenol (primary rival), Molex (Koch, private), Samtec, Foxconn

### Verdict: HOLD
TE Connectivity is a high-quality industrial company with genuine and growing AI data center exposure through its DDN business ($707M/quarter, +70% YoY) and emerging photonic capabilities (RAM Photonics, CPO sockets). However, it is NOT a photonics supply chain chokepoint in the same class as LITE, FN, or even Amphenol. The key differences vs Amphenol: (1) AI/DC is ~15% of TE revenue vs ~37%+ for Amphenol, (2) TE lacks Amphenol's 33% AI data center interconnect market share dominance, (3) ~60% of revenue is automotive/industrial with slower growth, and (4) operating margins trail Amphenol by ~700bps. The CPO and optical transceiver initiatives are promising but early-stage. At 18.6x forward P/E with ~17% analyst upside, the valuation is fair but doesn't offer a compelling entry for a photonics-focused portfolio. Watch the Q2 FY26 earnings on April 22 for updated AI revenue trajectory and margin expansion confirmation.
