# ENTG - Entegris, Inc.
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Score: 55/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Entegris is the leading integrated supplier of ultra-pure materials, filtration, and contamination control for semiconductor fabs -- providing CMP slurries, specialty chemicals, gas filtration, and fluid handling systems used in every advanced node fab including SiPh foundries. However, Entegris has no photonics-specific products, partnerships, or differentiation; its materials go into every semiconductor fab regardless of whether the fab makes logic chips, memory, or photonic ICs. This is a generic semiconductor materials infrastructure play, not a photonics chokepoint.

### Business Segments
**Materials Solutions (MS):** CMP slurries, advanced deposition materials (CVD/ALD precursors), ion implantation specialty gases, formulated etch/clean chemicals. Directly leveraged to chip complexity and node transitions.

**Advanced Purity Solutions (APS):** Filtration, purification, fluid handling, wafer transport (FOUPs). Benefits from shrinking node requirements and new fab construction. 63M+ CMP slurry filters deployed globally.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate-Strong (3/5)**

Entegris has a real competitive position but it's not a monopoly:
- **23% CMP slurry filter market share**: Leading but not dominant. Pall and Cobetter are viable alternatives.
- **Integrated purity system**: "Sells the chemical, the container, the filter, and the valve." Bundled solution creates switching costs.
- **Content per wafer growth**: Outgrows semiconductor market by 300-500bps due to exponential contamination sensitivity at advanced nodes.
- **80% consumables revenue**: Recurring, unit-driven. Less cyclical than equipment peers.
- **Qualification barriers**: 12-18 month qualification cycles for new chemical/filter suppliers at advanced fabs.
- **Risk**: DuPont (CMP pads), Merck (deposition materials), Shin-Etsu (specialty chemicals) are all credible competitors in individual product lines. Entegris's advantage is breadth, not monopoly in any single product.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream (Entegris's suppliers):**
- **Specialty chemical raw materials**: Bulk chemicals refined to ultra-pure grades.
- **Filter media and membrane suppliers**: Polymer and ceramic membrane inputs.

**Downstream (Entegris's customers):**
- **TSMC**: Largest customer. Every advanced node fab uses Entegris materials.
- **Samsung**: Memory and foundry fabs.
- **Intel**: Legacy and advanced node fabs.
- **Tower Semiconductor**: SiPh foundry uses Entegris-class materials (but not Entegris-specific).
- **GlobalFoundries**: SiPh foundry #2.
- **All other fabs**: Entegris materials are ubiquitous across the industry.

### Photonics Relevance Assessment
**Rating: TANGENTIAL -- Not a photonics chokepoint**

- Every SiPh fab (Tower, GlobalFoundries, STM, etc.) uses ultra-pure chemicals, CMP slurries, and filtration systems. Entegris supplies these. But so do DuPont, Merck, Pall, and others.
- There are NO photonics-specific Entegris products. CMP slurry for silicon photonics waveguides is the same CMP slurry used for CMOS logic. Filtration for SiPh fabs is the same filtration used for memory fabs.
- No photonics partnerships, no CPO materials programs, no silicon photonics-specific announcements in any public disclosures.
- SiPh foundry revenue (Tower ~$1.6B, GFS SiPh ~$200M) represents <1% of total semiconductor fab materials demand. Entegris's exposure to photonics fabs is a rounding error.
- The photonics angle is: "photonics fabs need chemicals too." This is true but not investable as a photonics thesis.

### Catalysts
1. **Semiconductor recovery 2026**: Industry expected to grow 8-10%. Entegris consumables grow with wafer starts.
2. **Content per wafer at N2/N1**: CMP slurry content doubling from N3 to N2. GAA transistors increase materials intensity.
3. **Taiwan + Colorado Springs fabs**: New capacity qualifying in 2026, supporting growth.
4. **Debt paydown**: CMC acquisition debt being reduced, which should improve net margins and ROE.
5. **HBM contamination solutions**: Growing content in HBM manufacturing (IPA purifiers).

### Risks
1. **High leverage**: D/E 0.96 from CMC Corporation acquisition. Interest expense compresses net margins to 7.4%.
2. **Revenue decline**: FY2025 revenue was -3% YoY. Recovery is expected but not guaranteed.
3. **Not a monopoly**: 23% filter share means 77% is with competitors. No single product line is irreplaceable.
4. **Valuation**: 87x TTM P/E and 6.4x sales is expensive for a company with declining revenue and 7.4% net margins.
5. **Semiconductor cyclicality**: Despite 80% consumables mix, revenue still declined in the downturn.
6. **Goldman Sachs downgrade**: December 2025 downgrade citing semiconductor supply chain headwinds.

### Valuation
At $20.6B market cap on $3.2B revenue (6.4x sales), Entegris is priced as a premium materials franchise. If FY2026 revenue reaches $3.5B with 21% adjusted operating margin, operating income would be ~$735M, and with ~$400M interest expense and taxes, net income of ~$350-400M implies 52-59x P/E -- still expensive. The 30.8x forward P/E assumes significant earnings recovery. Fair value range: $100-120 on FY2026E earnings, suggesting the stock is near fair value or slightly overvalued.

Status: `FINAL`.
Entegris received $77M in CHIPS Act funding for its Colorado Springs, Colorado facility, which began service in October 2025.

### Verdict: HOLD
Entegris is a well-positioned semiconductor materials company with genuine competitive advantages in integrated purity solutions and content-per-wafer growth. However, it is NOT a photonics chokepoint -- there are no photonics-specific products, no silicon photonics differentiation, and SiPh fabs represent a negligible fraction of its customer base. The stock is expensive at 87x TTM P/E with declining revenue, high leverage from the CMC acquisition, and only 7.4% net margins. For the AI photonics universe, Entegris is a generic semiconductor infrastructure play that happens to supply materials to SiPh fabs along with every other fab on the planet.

### Price Target: N/A
Not initiating a price target as Entegris falls outside the core photonics chokepoint thesis. For broad semiconductor materials exposure, monitor content-per-wafer growth and debt reduction trajectory.
