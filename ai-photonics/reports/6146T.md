### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Disco Corporation is the world's dominant precision dicing and grinding equipment maker with ~75% global market share -- every semiconductor chip, including every silicon photonics IC, must be diced from the wafer into individual die using Disco equipment before it can be packaged into a transceiver. With ¥393B ($2.74B) revenue growing 28%, 38x P/E, record dividends (+34.5%), and direct exposure to AI-driven semiconductor volume (GPUs, memory, photonic ICs), Disco is the "invisible picks-and-shovels" play on all chip manufacturing including photonics. A Japanese hidden gem with 22% average annual dividend growth over 10 years.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Exceptional (5/5)**

Disco's moat is one of the deepest in semiconductor equipment:
- **75% global dicing/grinding market share**: Near-monopoly in precision wafer dicing and grinding. No competitor comes close.
- **Razor/blade model**: Sells machines AND consumable blades/wheels that must be replaced regularly. Recurring revenue stream.
- **"Kiru, Kezuru, Migaku"**: Cutting, grinding, polishing -- integrated capability no competitor replicates end-to-end.
- **Every chip needs dicing**: Whether it's an NVIDIA GPU, a Tower Semi SiPh IC, or a Lumentum EML laser -- it must be diced from the wafer using Disco equipment.
- **39% operating margin**: Best-in-class profitability for semi equipment.
- **AI-driven volume**: More AI chips = more wafers = more dicing = more Disco equipment and consumables.

### Supply Chain Position
**Upstream to ALL chip makers**:
- Tower Semiconductor (SiPh ICs) → diced by Disco
- TSMC (logic, AI chips) → diced by Disco
- Every InP laser wafer → diced by Disco
- Every memory chip (HBM) → diced by Disco
- Consumable blades create recurring revenue on every wafer ever diced

### Catalysts
1. **AI semiconductor volume surge**: More GPUs, more photonic ICs, more HBM = more dicing demand.
2. **Advanced packaging growth**: 2.5D/3D packaging (CoWoS) requires precision grinding -- Disco's specialty.
3. **SiPh wafer dicing**: As SiPh wafer starts increase 5x (Tower expansion), Disco dicing volumes grow proportionally.
4. **Consumable revenue acceleration**: More wafers in production = more blade replacements = recurring revenue growth.
5. **Dividend aristocrat**: 22% avg annual dividend growth for 10 years.

### Risks
1. **Japan-listed**: Limited foreign investor access and coverage.
2. **P/E of 38x**: Premium valuation for equipment company. Any cyclical downturn compresses multiple.
3. **Cyclical semiconductor equipment**: Disco's revenue follows the semiconductor capex cycle.
4. **Yen appreciation risk**: Revenue denominated in JPY; weakening customers in USD terms.
