# 2317.TW - Hon Hai Precision (Foxconn)
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 62/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Foxconn is the world's largest contract electronics manufacturer with NT$8.1T ($248B) revenue, and cloud/networking has surged from 30% to 40% of revenue in a single year, making it a direct proxy for hyperscale AI server spending. The company assembles NVIDIA GPU servers, builds the racks that house optical transceivers, and manufactures the infrastructure that every photonics company supplies into. Not a photonics company itself, but the largest single buyer of AI data center components globally.

### Supply Chain Relevance
**World's largest AI server assembler**:
- Buys optical transceivers from Innolight, Coherent, Lumentum for server interconnects
- Buys AEC cables from Credo for rack-to-rack connections
- Uses Amphenol connectors throughout every server
- Assembles on Foxconn production lines using ASMPT/Besi packaging equipment
- Ships to every hyperscaler (MSFT, GOOG, META, AMZN)

### Verdict: HOLD
Foxconn is the gravitational center of AI hardware manufacturing but not a photonics play itself. The 40% cloud/networking revenue share makes it a broad AI infrastructure proxy. At ~$60B market cap on $248B revenue, it trades at just 0.24x sales -- the cheapest large-cap in the AI supply chain by a wide margin. But EMS margins are structurally thin. For photonics exposure, upstream component companies (MTSI, CRDO, TSEM) offer better leverage.
