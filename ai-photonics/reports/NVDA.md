# NVDA - NVIDIA Corporation (Optical Networking Context)
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: N/A (Context note, not standalone photonics rating)

### Role in AI Photonics Supply Chain
NVIDIA is not just the customer -- it's becoming a major optical networking company through the Mellanox acquisition and own silicon photonics development:

**Products:**
- **Spectrum-X Photonics**: CPO-based Ethernet switches with SiPh (512 ports × 800G = 400Tbps). Available 2026.
- **Quantum-X Photonics**: CPO-based InfiniBand switches (144 ports × 800G). Available H2 2025.
- **LinkX Transceivers**: Full line of 10G-400G optical transceivers and AOCs.
- **ConnectX-8/9 SuperNICs**: 800Gb/s network adapters for GPU interconnect.

**Performance Claims:**
- 3.5x more power efficient than traditional pluggable optics
- 63x greater signal integrity
- 5x power efficiency from CPO integration

**Strategic Investments:**
- $2B each into Lumentum and Coherent for optical technology partnerships
- $4B total optical ecosystem investment

### Why This Matters
NVIDIA's entry into CPO-based switches with own silicon photonics means:
1. **Demand signal**: NVIDIA is the largest single buyer of optical transceivers for its GPU clusters
2. **Technology direction**: CPO becoming mandatory for next-gen AI clusters validates Tower Semi, Soitec, Broadcom CPO thesis
3. **Supplier relationships**: Lumentum lasers in Spectrum-X, Coherent SiPh collaboration
4. **Vertical integration risk**: NVIDIA could eventually disintermediate some optical component suppliers

### Note
NVIDIA is not rated as a standalone photonics play (it's a $3T+ GPU company). But its $4B optical investment and CPO switch launches are the most important demand signal in the entire photonics ecosystem.
