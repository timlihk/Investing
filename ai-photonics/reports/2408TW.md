### Relevance to Photonics Thesis
This company does not sit in the optical-interconnect value chain mapped by the repo. Nanya is a DRAM manufacturer. Its relevance here is indirect at best: AI servers need memory, and hyperscale capex can lift DRAM demand. That is useful macro confirmation, but it is not the same thing as owning a photonics chokepoint.

### Why It Appeared In The DIGITIMES Article
DIGITIMES ranked Nanya second among Taiwan semiconductor manufacturers in cumulative 2025 growth. The article itself attributed that performance to DRAM recovery, especially stronger DDR4 demand, rather than to CPO or silicon-photonics exposure. That distinction matters; the stock was mentioned in the same list, but for a different reason than LandMark, IntelliEPI, or GCS.

### Catalysts
1. **DRAM cycle recovery**: Nanya's Q3 2025 results showed strong sequential and year-over-year improvement.
2. **Server and AI memory demand**: management highlighted AI and general cloud servers as demand drivers into 2026.
3. **DDR5 / process migration**: the company disclosed progress on 16Gb DDR5 and next-node process development.

### Risks
1. **Outside thesis**: even if the stock works, it is likely to work for memory-cycle reasons, not optical-infrastructure reasons.
2. **Commodity economics**: DRAM pricing cycles are far less chokepoint-like than the bottlenecks this repo tries to isolate.
3. **HBM / premium-memory gap**: AI memory upside increasingly concentrates in HBM and advanced nodes, where Nanya is not the obvious leader.
