# 2455.TW - Visual Photonics Epitaxy (VPEC)
## Date: 2026-03-26
## Score: 63/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
VPEC is a Taiwan-listed compound semiconductor epitaxial wafer company making InP photodetectors, GaAs VCSELs, and DFB laser epiwafers — the critical layer between InP substrates and finished laser chips. At $955M market cap on $100M TTM revenue, VPEC is more reasonably valued than LandMark (67x sales) at ~10x sales. The company's InP photodetector epiwafers for 800G high-speed transmission are seeing double-digit annual growth. Part of the Taiwan epitaxial trio (with LandMark and IntelliEPI) capturing the 70% InP supply gap.

### Moat: Moderate (3/5)
- Established compound semiconductor epiwafer expertise (since 1996)
- InP photodetector epiwafers are essential receiver components in every optical transceiver
- Part of the Taiwan epitaxial cluster with government support
- Competes with LandMark and IntelliEPI in Taiwan, IQE globally

### Supply Chain Position
- **Upstream**: Buys InP/GaAs substrates from AXT, JX Metals
- **Downstream**: Sells epiwafers to photodetector and laser chip makers
- **Key product**: InP photodetectors for 800G transceivers (receiver side)

### Verdict: HOLD
VPEC is the most reasonably valued of the Taiwan InP epiwafer trio at ~10x sales (vs LandMark 67x, IntelliEPI at higher multiples). The InP photodetector epiwafer business directly serves the 800G/1.6T receiver market. More focused on the receiver (photodetector) side vs IntelliEPI (transmitter/PIN) and LandMark (both), providing portfolio diversification.
