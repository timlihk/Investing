# 6967.T - Shinko Electric Industries (DELISTED)
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Score: N/A (Delisted)

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Shinko Electric Industries was a leading FC-BGA substrate manufacturer and one of the few companies actively developing optical waveguide substrates for co-packaged optics (CPO). The company was taken private via a JIC-led consortium tender offer at JPY 5,920/share in early 2025, with delisting completed on June 6, 2025. Shinko is no longer investable as a public equity, though it remains relevant as a private company in the CPO substrate supply chain with potential re-listing after corporate restructuring.

### Delisting Details
- **Tender Offer Price**: JPY 5,920/share
- **Buyer**: JICC-04 LTD (JIC Capital consortium including DNP and Mitsui Chemicals)
- **Acceptance**: 93.89% of shares tendered by March 18, 2025
- **Delisting Date**: June 6, 2025 (from Tokyo Stock Exchange)
- **Re-listing plan**: JIC's stated policy is to re-list after business growth and corporate value enhancement
- Source: [JPX Delisting Notice](https://www.jpx.co.jp/english/news/1023/20250520-11.html)
- Source: [Digitimes: Shinko delisting, DNP/Mitsui partnership](https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20250321PD206/shinko-electric-mitsui-chemicals-materials-partnership-fujitsu.html)

### CPO Substrate Technology (Why This Mattered)
Shinko was one of the most interesting companies in the CPO supply chain because it was developing **FC-BGA substrates with integrated optical waveguides** -- a key enabling technology for co-packaged optics:

- **Optical waveguide substrates**: Shinko's product page described substrates that combine electrical and optical signal transmission within a single package substrate. This is critical for CPO where optical engines must be co-located with ASICs.
- **Optical chiplet assembly**: Shinko was developing assembly technologies for integrating optical chiplets onto FC-BGA substrates.
- Source: [Shinko: Substrate with Optical Waveguides](https://www.shinko.co.jp/english/product/package/substrate/co-pkg.php)
- Source: [Shinko: Optical Chiplet/Co-Packaged Optics](https://www.shinko.co.jp/english/product/package/assembly/cpo1.php)

### Pre-Delisting Market Position
- **FC-BGA market share**: Top 5 globally alongside Unimicron, Ibiden, AT&S, and Nan Ya PCB. The top 5 controlled ~60% of global ABF substrate capacity, with no single player exceeding 18%.
- **ABF substrate market**: Growing from $4.89B (2024) to $9.55B by 2032 at 10.6% CAGR.
- **FY ending March 2024 revenue**: JPY 209.97B (down 26.7% YoY on weak PC/standard server demand)
- **AI bright spot**: Management noted AI-related substrate demand was strong even as consumer/enterprise demand weakened.
- **Osaka plant**: New fab commissioned in 2023, boosting ABF output by 30%.

### Chokepoint Assessment: DELISTED -- NOT INVESTABLE
Shinko was a genuinely interesting CPO substrate candidate with real optical waveguide technology. The FC-BGA substrate market itself is an oligopoly (top 5 = 60% share) but not a tight chokepoint -- there are 5+ qualified suppliers. The optical waveguide substrate for CPO was more differentiated but pre-production. The delisting removes it from consideration.

**Key takeaway for the chokepoint thesis**: The CPO substrate layer is worth monitoring. If optical waveguide substrates become critical for CPO, the supplier who masters this first could have a temporary chokepoint. Watch Ibiden (4062.T) and the JOINT3 consortium (led by Resonac) for panel-level organic interposers.
