# STM - STMicroelectronics NV
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 62/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
STMicroelectronics has quietly entered high-volume production of its PIC100 silicon photonics platform for 800G and 1.6T optical transceivers in hyperscale data centers and AI clusters -- a major new entrant in the SiPh foundry market alongside Tower Semi, GlobalFoundries, UMC, and X-FAB. With plans to quadruple SiPh production capacity by 2027 and already supporting co-packaged optics (CPO) development, STM adds another foundry option for fabless photonics companies. However, at ~$25B+ market cap, SiPh is a small new business line within STM's diversified semiconductor portfolio (automotive, industrial, IoT).

### Moat: Emerging in SiPh (2.5/5)
- PIC100 is a proven platform now in high-volume production -- not just a development effort.
- 4x capacity expansion by 2027 signals serious commitment.
- But Tower Semi has 85% SiPh share and STM is starting from near-zero market share.
- Diversified semiconductor company -- SiPh is one of many product lines.

### Supply Chain Position
**5th SiPh foundry entrant** (after Tower Semi, GlobalFoundries, UMC, X-FAB):
- Fabless photonics customers can now choose from 5 SiPh foundries
- STM's European fabs provide geographic diversification
- Competition reduces Tower Semi's monopoly pricing power

### Verdict: HOLD
STM's PIC100 SiPh production is a significant supply chain development -- adding a 5th major foundry option and quadrupling capacity by 2027. But SiPh is still a tiny business within $13B revenue, and Tower Semi's 85% share won't be displaced quickly. For pure SiPh foundry exposure, Tower Semi (TSEM) remains the higher-conviction play. STM offers diversified European semiconductor exposure with SiPh optionality.

**Final Score: 62/100**
