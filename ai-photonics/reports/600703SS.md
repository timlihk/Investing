# 600703.SS - Sanan Optoelectronics
## Date: 2026-03-26
## Score: 60/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Sanan Optoelectronics is China's largest compound semiconductor company ($12.2B market cap) with InP substrate production capacity of 10,000 6-inch substrates/month at its Wuhan facility — a key Chinese player in closing the 70% InP supply gap. Products have entered Huawei's supply chain. Raised CNY 6.5B for expansion across GaN, GaAs, SiC, and InP. While primarily an LED/display company, the InP and optical communication chip divisions are the high-growth AI-adjacent segments.

### Moat: Moderate (2.5/5)
- China's largest compound semiconductor company with broad platform (InP, GaN, GaAs, SiC)
- InP substrate production at scale addresses China's domestic supply chain security needs
- Huawei relationship provides stable demand floor
- But primarily LED/display company — InP/optical is a smaller segment
- Competes with AXT (US/China) and JX Metals (Japan) in InP substrates

### Supply Chain Position
- **Chinese alternative to AXT**: Sanan provides domestic InP substrates for Chinese transceiver makers (Innolight, Eoptolink, Accelink)
- **Reduces China's dependence on imported InP**: Strategic importance for Chinese semiconductor self-sufficiency
- **Huawei supply chain**: Direct exposure to China's largest telecom/networking company

### Verdict: HOLD
Sanan is the Chinese national champion in compound semiconductors with meaningful InP substrate capacity. The 70% global InP supply gap benefits all qualified suppliers. However, InP is a small fraction of the $2.5B LED/display-dominated revenue, and Shanghai listing limits foreign access. For pure InP substrate exposure, AXT (AXTI) and JX Metals (5016.T) are more direct plays.

**Final Score: 60/100**
