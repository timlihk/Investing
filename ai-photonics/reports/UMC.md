# UMC / 2303.TW - United Microelectronics Corporation
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 59/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
UMC is Taiwan's #2 foundry (behind TSMC) that has licensed imec's iSiPP300 silicon photonics process technology, entering the SiPh foundry market with risk production in 2026-2027. This creates a third major SiPh foundry option alongside Tower Semiconductor (85% share) and GlobalFoundries, leveraging UMC's existing SOI wafer processing and 8-inch SiPh experience. While early-stage in photonics, UMC's entry validates the SiPh foundry market as a significant opportunity and could gradually diversify the supply chain away from Tower's near-monopoly.

### Moat: Emerging in SiPh (2.5/5)
- Large, established foundry with SOI and 8-inch SiPh experience.
- imec iSiPP300 is a proven, advanced platform with CPO compatibility.
- But Tower Semi has 85% SiPh share and 5x expansion underway. UMC is years behind in qualification.

### Supply Chain Position
- **New SiPh foundry entrant**: Competes with Tower Semi (TSEM) and GlobalFoundries (GFS)
- **Uses**: Soitec (SOI.PA) photonics-SOI wafers (likely)
- **Customers**: Working with "several new customers" for PIC chips
- **Technology partner**: imec (Belgium research institute)

### Verdict: HOLD
UMC's SiPh entry is strategically important -- it creates supply chain diversification for fabless photonics companies currently dependent on Tower Semi. However, risk production in 2026-2027 means meaningful SiPh revenue is years away. Tower Semi's 85% share and 5x expansion make it the entrenched leader. UMC is the long-term diversification play for the SiPh foundry market, not a near-term photonics investment.
