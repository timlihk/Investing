# APH - Amphenol Corporation
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 78/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
Amphenol is the dominant interconnect company riding the AI data center wave -- $23.1B revenue (+52%), 33% market share in AI data center interconnects, and Communications Solutions segment growing 78% driven by hyperscaler demand. The CommScope CCS acquisition ($4.1B annual revenue) adds fiber optic interconnects specifically for AI/data center, making Amphenol the largest physical connectivity provider in the world. Record 27.5% operating margins demonstrate this isn't just revenue growth -- it's profitable, scaled, franchise-quality growth.

### Latest Official Management Outlook (2026-01-28)
Source: [Amphenol Reports Record Fourth Quarter and Full Year 2025 Results](https://investors.amphenol.com/news-and-events/news-details/2026/Amphenol-Reports-Record-Fourth-Quarter-and-Full-Year-2025-Results/default.aspx)

- Amphenol reported Q4 2025 sales of $6.4B, up 49% YoY in dollars and 37% organically, with 27.5% adjusted operating margin. Management highlighted exceptional organic growth in IT datacom.
- For Q1 2026, management guided to $6.90B-$7.00B of sales and adjusted diluted EPS of $0.91-$0.93. That outlook includes about $900M of revenue and $0.02 of EPS accretion from the CCS acquisition.
- Demand read-through: management is explicitly saying the electronics build-out, including AI-linked datacom, is broad-based rather than isolated. That keeps Amphenol relevant as a physical-layer beneficiary rather than just an M&A roll-up.
- Supply-side read-through: the key lever here is acquisition-enabled breadth, not scarcity. CCS expands fiber and cable capability, but investors should frame Amphenol as a scale-and-execution winner rather than a hard chokepoint with constrained supply.
