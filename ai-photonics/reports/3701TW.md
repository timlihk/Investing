# 3701.TW - FIC Global (FICG)
## Date: 2026-03-26
## Score: 62/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
FIC Global is a Taiwan-listed optical transceiver packaging and assembly company with 17.5% market share in 800G/1.6T transceivers and 20% in 400G — a significant under-covered player that's been hiding in plain sight. The company demonstrated 3.2T optical transceiver products entering validation stage at OFC 2026 and APE 2026, with advanced flip-chip packaging for high-speed transceivers. Over 50% of revenue now comes from optical transceivers, with integrated optical packaging capabilities spanning the 800G-to-3.2T roadmap.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate (3/5)**
- 17.5% market share in 800G/1.6T transceiver packaging is meaningful — sits between the Chinese Big 3 (Innolight/Eoptolink/Accelink) and Western module makers.
- Flip-chip packaging for optical transceivers is an advanced capability that takes years to qualify.
- 3.2T validation positions FICG for the next upgrade cycle.
- Low margins (5.8% EBITDA, 1.1% net) are a concern — packaging is competitive.
- Taiwan listing limits foreign coverage.

### Supply Chain Position
- **Competes with**: Fabrinet (FN) in optical assembly, TFC Optical (300394.SZ) in Chinese OSA
- **Supplies to**: Hyperscalers and transceiver module makers
- **Products**: Optical transceiver packaging, PCB assembly, system integration

### Catalysts
1. **3.2T products entering validation** — early mover advantage for next-gen transceivers
2. **OFC 2026 showcase** — raised profile with industry
3. **Optical revenue growing to >50%** — transitioning to pure-play
4. **800G/1.6T volume ramp** — 17.5% share benefits from 63M unit shipments in 2026

### Risks
1. **Low margins** — 1.1% net margin and 5.8% EBITDA margin are thin
2. **Small market cap** (~$300M) — limited institutional coverage
3. **Competition** from Fabrinet (NVIDIA sole-source) and Chinese OSA companies
4. **Taiwan listing** — limited foreign access

### Verdict: HOLD
FICG is an under-covered Taiwan optical transceiver packaging company with meaningful 800G/1.6T market share (17.5%) and 3.2T products in validation. The optical business is growing to majority of revenue. However, thin margins and small market cap limit near-term investment appeal. Worth monitoring as a potential acquisition target or as the 3.2T cycle materializes.
