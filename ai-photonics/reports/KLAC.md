# KLAC - KLA Corporation
## Date: 2026-04-12
## Score: 66/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
KLA Corporation is the dominant process control and inspection equipment company with ~56-60% market share in semiconductor metrology/inspection and ~75-80% in patterned wafer inspection. Every silicon photonics wafer fabricated at Tower, GlobalFoundries, UMC, STM, or X-FAB runs through KLA inspection and metrology tools for defect detection, overlay measurement, and yield management. At $228B market cap on $12.7B revenue, KLA is the "quality police" of all semiconductor manufacturing -- including SiPh -- but photonics is a tiny fraction of its business, similar to ASML, AMAT, and LRCX.

### Key Question: Is KLA a Photonics-Specific Chokepoint?

**Answer: No -- KLA is a semiconductor-wide chokepoint that happens to be essential for SiPh, not a photonics-specific play.**

The honest comparison to ASML (25/25), AMAT (18/25), and LRCX (18/25):
- **ASML**: Monopoly lithography. Every SiPh waveguide is patterned with ASML DUV tools. No alternative exists.
- **AMAT**: Duopoly deposition. Every SiPh thin film is grown with AMAT (or Lam) tools. Plus direct GF Singapore photonics fab collaboration.
- **LRCX**: Duopoly etch. Every SiPh waveguide is etched with Lam (or AMAT) tools.
- **KLA**: Dominant inspection/metrology. Every SiPh wafer is inspected with KLA tools for defects, overlay error, CD measurement. KLA's share (~56-60%) is higher than AMAT or LRCX in their respective segments.

KLA's photonics angle is arguably stronger than AMAT or LRCX because:
1. **Higher market share**: ~56-60% overall, ~75-80% in patterned wafer inspection vs AMAT/LRCX at ~30-35% in their segments
2. **Process control intensity is rising**: KLA targets 9% of wafer fab capex by 2030 (up from 7.4% in 2025), driven by defect sensitivity in photonic waveguides and advanced packaging
3. **Advanced packaging quadrupling**: KLA grew from 10% to ~50% advanced packaging market share in 4 years, with $950M advanced packaging revenue in CY2025 (+70% YoY). CPO/SiPh packaging requires KLA inspection.
4. **Substrate support**: KLA explicitly supports SOI, GaAs, InP, SiC, GaN, and epitaxial wafer inspection -- the full photonics substrate stack

But KLA does NOT have:
- A photonics-specific product line (unlike FormFactor's Keystone SiPh acquisition)
- A dedicated SiPh inspection tool (optical waveguide-specific)
- Disclosed SiPh-specific revenue (it's lumped into foundry/logic)

### CHIPS Act Update (2026-04-12)
Status: `NONE`.

No direct CHIPS incentives award identified for KLA. As an equipment supplier (not a fab operator), KLA benefits indirectly from CHIPS Act through increased tool orders from subsidized fabs (Tower, GF, Intel, etc.) but does not receive direct subsidies.

### Verdict: HOLD

KLA is arguably the strongest chokepoint among the "Big 4" semiconductor equipment companies (ASML/AMAT/LRCX/KLA) after ASML, with higher market share in its segment (~56-60%) than AMAT (~30%) or LRCX (~30%) in theirs. The financial profile is exceptional -- 62% gross margins, 41% operating margins, $3.7B FCF, 100% ROE. The 2030 targets ($26B revenue, $84 EPS, 45-47% operating margins) are credible and well-supported by AI/HBM/advanced packaging trends.

However, KLA is NOT a photonics-specific play. Like ASML, AMAT, and LRCX, KLA benefits from SiPh fab expansion but photonics is a tiny, undisclosed fraction of revenue. The advanced packaging segment ($950M, +70% YoY) is the closest proxy for CPO/SiPh relevance but covers all packaging types.

The valuation is the problem: at 50x trailing P/E, 36x forward, and 18x sales, KLA is near its all-time high and ABOVE analyst consensus. The market has fully recognized the AI process control story. For this portfolio's photonics-specific thesis, KLA provides no incremental edge over what ASML/AMAT/LRCX already cover as generic semi equipment context notes.

**Where KLA fits**: Context note alongside ASML (25/25), AMAT (18/25), LRCX (18/25). KLA's 20/25 chokepoint score is actually the highest of the non-ASML equipment trio, reflecting its dominant market share. But for actionable photonics investments, Tower (85% SiPh foundry), Soitec (sole SOI supplier), or CRDO (88% AEC monopoly) offer far more targeted exposure.
