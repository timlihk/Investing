# 8258.TW - IntelliEPI (IET)
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 62/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
IntelliEPI is Taiwan's fastest-growing III-V compound semiconductor epitaxial wafer maker (+53.8% revenue growth), specializing in InP, GaAs, and GaSb epiwafers using proprietary MBE (molecular beam epitaxy) technology. Its InP 200G PIN photodetector products have become the main revenue driver as AI data center optical communication scales, and a strategic share-swap with EZconn creates an end-to-end silicon photonics CPO capability from epitaxy to module-level integration. A genuine under-discovered Taiwan photonics play at the intersection of InP epiwafers and CPO.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate (3/5)**

- **InP epitaxy expertise**: One of few Taiwan companies with InP MBE capability for optical communication.
- **200G PIN photodetectors**: InP-based receivers are critical for 800G/1.6T transceivers.
- **EZconn CPO integration**: Share-swap creates vertical integration from epitaxy to optical module for CPO -- unique in Taiwan supply chain.
- **Small scale**: ~$30M revenue. Competing with much larger IQE (UK) and AXT (US/China) in epiwafers.

### Supply Chain Position
```
IntelliEPI (InP/GaAs epiwafers via MBE) → optical device fabrication → PIN photodetectors for transceiver RX
                                           ↓ (EZconn partnership)
                                           CPO optical module integration
```
- **Upstream competitor to**: IQE (IQE.L), AXT (AXTI), JX Advanced Metals (5016.T)
- **Downstream via EZconn**: Optical component + module assembly for CPO
- **Key advantage**: Taiwan-based, non-China supply chain for InP epiwafers

### Catalysts
1. **InP 200G PIN product ramp**: Main revenue driver, directly addressing 1.6T transceiver receiver demand.
2. **EZconn CPO integration**: End-to-end SiPh CPO capability from epitaxy to module is highly differentiated.
3. **InP shortage**: Global InP substrate/epiwafer shortage benefits all established suppliers.
4. **Taiwan SiPh/CPO ecosystem**: Government and industry investing heavily in Taiwan photonics.
5. **AMD $8.64B Taiwan SiPh R&D center**: Validates Taiwan as a photonics manufacturing hub.

### Risks
1. **Micro-cap scale**: ~$30M revenue limits institutional interest.
2. **Taiwan-only listing**: Limited foreign investor access.
3. **InP material shortage**: Could constrain IntelliEPI's own production growth.
4. **Competition**: IQE, AXT, and JX Advanced Metals are larger and more established.
5. **EZconn integration risk**: Share-swap adds complexity.

### Verdict: HOLD
IntelliEPI is one of the most genuinely under-discovered names in this research -- a Taiwan InP epiwafer specialist with 53.8% growth, 200G PIN products as the main driver, and a unique EZconn CPO integration pathway. The combination of InP epitaxy + CPO module capability in a single supply chain is rare. However, ~$30M revenue and Taiwan-only listing limit near-term investability. Monitor for revenue crossing $50M threshold and EZconn CPO production qualification.

**Final Score: 62/100**
