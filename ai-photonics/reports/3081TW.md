# 3081.TWO - LandMark Optoelectronics
## Date: 2026-03-26
## Score: 65/100

### Thesis (2-3 sentences)
LandMark Optoelectronics is a Taiwan-listed InP and GaAs epitaxial wafer specialist that directly benefits from the 70% InP supply gap driving the AI optical transceiver bottleneck. At $4.77B market cap on $70.6M TTM revenue, the stock has been dramatically re-rated by the market as InP shortage awareness grows. The company makes the InP/GaAs epiwafers that become the laser chips inside every 800G/1.6T transceiver — sitting alongside IntelliEPI and VPEC as the Taiwan epitaxial trio capturing the InP supply crisis.

### Moat Analysis
**Rating: Moderate-Strong (3.5/5)**
- One of the leading Taiwan InP/GaAs epitaxial wafer suppliers alongside IntelliEPI and VPEC
- Taiwan commands ~70% of global epitaxial wafer manufacturing — LandMark is a key player in this cluster
- InP wafer growth expertise takes years to develop — high barrier to entry
- The 70% InP supply gap means every qualified InP epiwafer supplier has strong pricing power through 2027+
- Competition from IQE (UK), AXT (US/China), and JX Metals (Japan) in substrates, but epiwafer is a different (downstream) step

### Supply Chain Position
```
AXT/JX Metals (InP substrates) → LandMark (InP epitaxial growth via MOCVD) → Lumentum/Coherent/Sivers (laser chip fabrication) → transceivers
```
- **Upstream**: Buys InP substrates from AXT, JX Metals, Sumitomo
- **Downstream**: Sells InP epiwafers to laser chip makers (Lumentum, Coherent, Sivers, device companies)
- **Competes with**: IntelliEPI (8258.TW), VPEC (2455.TW), IQE (IQE.L)

### Catalysts
1. **70% InP supply gap**: Global InP demand is 2M pieces vs 600K capacity. Every InP supplier is fully booked to 2026+
2. **Pricing power**: Shortage-driven pricing allows margin expansion
3. **1.6T/3.2T transceiver ramp**: Each new transceiver generation needs more/better InP epiwafers
4. **Taiwan epitaxial cluster**: Government + industry investing in Taiwan as photonics manufacturing hub

### Risks
1. **$4.77B market cap on $70.6M revenue = 67x sales** — extreme valuation
2. **Small revenue base** — still a small company in absolute terms
3. **Taiwan listing** — limited foreign investor access
4. **Cyclical epiwafer demand** — if AI capex slows, InP demand could normalize
5. **AXT capacity expansion** — Beijing-based competitor expanding aggressively

### Verdict: HOLD
LandMark is a genuine under-discovered InP epiwafer specialist sitting at the heart of the 70% InP supply gap. The supply chain position is strong and the shortage is real. However, at 67x trailing sales, the market has already aggressively priced in the InP shortage story. Better value may exist at IntelliEPI (8258.TW) which trades at much lower multiples with similar positioning.

**Final Score: 65/100**
