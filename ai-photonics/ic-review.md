# AI Photonics IC Review

Date: 2026-03-25

This memo is an independent committee read of the existing `ai-photonics` project. It uses the repo's reports and `results.tsv` as inputs, but it does not take the internal score, target, or narrative at face value. Committee bias: favor durable bottlenecks, penalize valuation stretch and thesis leakage, and treat design-win slides as weaker evidence than repeatable margins and customer dependence.

## Committee-Level View

- The strongest underwritable ideas here are mostly toll collectors rather than the most promotional optics names: `ANET`, `AVGO`, `CRDO`, `MTSI`, `TSEM`, and `APH` look like the cleanest businesses.
- The most common flaw in the file is thesis dilution. Many companies do touch AI photonics, but the exposure is too small relative to the group, or the economics look more cyclical than chokepoint-like.
- The China set contains some of the most economically compelling winners, especially `300308.SZ`, but committee sizing would have to be capped because access, export controls, and geopolitical risk can dominate fundamentals.
- CHIPS grants help funding and political positioning, but they do not by themselves create a moat. For `GFS`, `AMKR`, `COHR`, `MTSI`, `GLW`, `INTC`, `AMAT`, `NOK`, and `SIVE.ST`, the grant is supportive rather than thesis-defining.

## Systems, Networking, and Assembly

- `ANET` Arista Networks. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: This is still one of the best names in the file because it monetizes AI optical demand through a proven switching and software franchise rather than needing one transceiver architecture to win. Top risks: hyperscaler insourcing, AI capex digestion, or optical intensity per cluster growing slower than current expectations.
- `AVGO` Broadcom. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: Broadcom has real chokepoint characteristics across switching, custom AI silicon, and optical DSP, so the thesis is stronger than a generic "AI beneficiary" label. Top risks: valuation, customer concentration in a handful of hyperscalers, and the possibility that optical upside remains too small versus the broader AI silicon story.
- `CSCO` Cisco Systems. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Acacia and Silicon One make Cisco more relevant than the market credits, but the committee would treat it as a diversified networking incumbent, not a pure photonics winner. Top risks: continued share loss to Arista, slower AI monetization, and optical strength being buried by the rest of the portfolio.
- `CIEN` Ciena Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The optical networking position is credible and backlog helps, but this remains closer to a carrier-optics and enterprise networking story than a hard AI photonics bottleneck. Top risks: telecom spending cyclicality, margin improvement failing to materialize, and hyperscaler optical growth not fully offsetting weaker legacy demand.
- `FN` Fabrinet. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The contract manufacturing role is real and attractive because Fabrinet wins regardless of which optical module vendor wins, and the balance sheet lowers underwriting risk. Top risks: customer concentration, limited pricing power as a manufacturer, and eventual dual-sourcing reducing sole-source economics.
- `CLS` Celestica. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Celestica looks more interesting than the headline `HOLD` because the valuation is modest and the company is embedded in AI system assembly, but it is still a manufacturing partner rather than a protected choke point. Top risks: low-margin EMS economics, program concentration, and a fast reversal if hyperscaler build plans pause.
- `APH` Amphenol Corporation. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: This is a strong committee-quality name because every AI rack needs interconnect, and Amphenol's economics suggest it has genuine pricing power rather than just volume growth. Top risks: the optical angle is still part of a wider connector portfolio, and some of the current multiple already prices in a long AI build cycle.
- `2317.TW` Hon Hai Precision (Foxconn). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Foxconn is a huge AI hardware proxy and an important read-through on demand, but it is not a photonics choke point and does not control economics at the optical layer. Top risks: low margins, heavy customer bargaining power, and broad hardware-cycle volatility.
- `2382.TW` Quanta Computer. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Quanta is a cleaner AI server proxy than many peers, but the committee would still classify it as an assembly beneficiary rather than a differentiated photonics exposure. Top risks: order normalization after the Blackwell wave, customer concentration, and limited structural moat versus other ODMs.
- `3231.TW` Wistron Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Wistron's growth is impressive, but the committee would discount it as a capacity-booking story until margins prove durable. Top risks: very high program concentration, a potential digestion cycle after current server ramps, and the same ODM commoditization risk seen across the group.
- `NOK` Nokia Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Infinera improves the optical case and the CHIPS-linked angle is helpful, but this is still a telecom infrastructure company where the photonics thesis can be drowned by other divisions. Top risks: integration risk, carrier spending weakness, and a slower-than-expected return on the Infinera acquisition.
- `VRT` Vertiv Holdings. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Vertiv is a valid AI infrastructure holding and a strong demand signal, but the committee would not call it a photonics investment except in the loosest sense. Top risks: valuation after a sharp run, backlog normalization, and the possibility that cooling becomes less supply constrained.

## Transceivers, Modules, and Optical Content

- `COHR` Coherent Corp. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Coherent has one of the broadest and most strategically valuable optical portfolios in the file, and the CHIPS support helps de-risk capex, but the stock already assumes a lot of execution. Top risks: integration complexity across multiple architectures, margin conversion lagging revenue, and a pull-forward in demand.
- `LITE` Lumentum Holdings. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The committee agrees Lumentum is a real laser bottleneck, especially in high-end EML, but the current valuation leaves little room for a pause or competitive catch-up. Top risks: supply normalization, customer diversification away from Lumentum, and a slower mix shift toward the highest-value products.
- `AAOI` Applied Optoelectronics. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The U.S.-manufacturing and non-China angle is interesting, and the company has real upside if 1.6T volumes convert cleanly, but this remains a turnaround, not a proven franchise. Top risks: profitability still not established, execution on new ramps, and customer concentration in a few design wins.
- `300308.SZ` Zhongji Innolight. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: On business quality alone this may be the strongest optical module company in the project, and the committee would take the thesis seriously despite the listing. Top risks: export controls, restricted investor access, and the chance that geopolitics overwhelms operating strength.
- `300502.SZ` Eoptolink Technology. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Eoptolink is a credible second source in a strong market, but the committee would not pay near-leader multiples for a name with less obvious moat and the same China risk stack. Top risks: competition with Innolight, valuation stretch, and dependency on a narrow AI optics window.
- `002281.SZ` Accelink Technologies. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Broad product coverage is positive, but the repo does not yet show evidence that Accelink owns the most valuable hyperscaler relationships. Top risks: heavier telecom/domestic-China mix, weaker pricing power, and lower quality growth than the top transceiver names.
- `002475.SZ` Luxshare Precision Industry. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Luxshare is clearly relevant to AI hardware and optics, but the optical business looks too small relative to the whole company to justify a dedicated thesis. Top risks: thesis leakage into Apple and broader assembly exposure, geopolitical risk, and limited transparency on photonics profitability.
- `300394.SZ` Suzhou TFC Optical Communication. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The packaging-bridge role is plausible and useful, but the committee needs more proof that TFC captures attractive economics rather than acting as an interchangeable subcontractor. Top risks: commoditization, customer concentration, and China market structure risk.
- `000936.SZ` Jiangsu Huaxicun (Source Photonics parent). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The Source Photonics stake gives genuine exposure, but the holdco structure and limited transparency are big enough to keep the committee away. Top risks: governance, capital allocation, and a persistent discount between operating value and listed value.
- `000988.SZ` Huagong Tech (HG Tech). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The company has enough optical relevance to stay on the list, but the committee sees it as an AI-adjacent industrial/laser hybrid rather than a direct photonics choke point. Top risks: mixed business quality across segments, limited evidence of durable AI optics earnings, and China cyclicality.
- `300548.SZ` Broadex Technologies. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Broadex looks like a capable mid-tier component supplier, but nothing in the file suggests it has locked up a bottleneck layer. Top risks: price competition, smaller customer relationships, and the normal China access issues.
- `0877.HK` O-Net Technologies Group. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The partner network is interesting and the passive/component layer matters, but the committee would need clearer evidence of scale economics before calling it underwritten. Top risks: competition, execution, and the gap between partnership announcements and sustained earnings.

## Connectivity Silicon, Lasers, and Device Suppliers

- `CRDO` Credo Technology Group. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: This looks like a true committee favorite because the company sits in a narrow, high-value layer of AI connectivity and has better economics than most optical hardware names. Top risks: customer concentration, architecture shifts away from AEC or current DSP choices, and volatility from a short product cycle.
- `MTSI` MACOM Technology Solutions. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: MACOM is one of the best ideas in the project because it sells the analog content inside many module architectures and does not need to pick one winner at the system level. Top risks: concentration in a few large customers, potential insourcing by larger silicon vendors, and the usual optical cycle volatility despite the CHIPS support.
- `MRVL` Marvell Technology. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: The committee sees real upside in Marvell's willingness to bet on photonic fabrics and custom AI silicon, but this is a broad data center semiconductor story, not a pure chokepoint. Top risks: AI custom program timing, integration risk around photonic acquisitions, and valuation if the market cools on AI networking.
- `SMTC` Semtech Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The LPO/LRO angle is intellectually interesting because it challenges the current power budget of optical links, but the committee would treat it as an architecture bet rather than a certainty. Top risks: DSP-based pluggables remain the standard, adoption takes longer than expected, and debt or legacy business noise returns.
- `MXL` MaxLinear Inc. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: There is optionality if Rushmore and related products win share, but the committee sees a small player fighting much larger, better-resourced competitors. Top risks: design wins fail to scale, pricing pressure, and a turnaround that remains perpetually one cycle away.
- `6503.T` Mitsubishi Electric. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The EML position is real and strategically important, but a conglomerate wrapper means the committee cannot underwrite this as a clean optics idea. Top risks: capital allocation elsewhere in the group, the optical piece staying immaterial, and competition closing any component lead.
- `5802.T` Sumitomo Electric Industries. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: This is another case where the technology exposure exists but the listed security is too broad to express the thesis precisely. Top risks: the company lags the very top tier in cutting-edge optical components, and non-optical segments will dominate the stock's behavior.
- `6777.T` Santec Holdings. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Santec's niche is real, but it is a narrow product company rather than a broad choke point, so committee underwriting would be cautious. Top risks: small scale, limited liquidity, customer concentration, and substitution if system architectures change.
- `8258.TW` IntelliEPI (IET). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: This is one of the more interesting underfollowed names in the file because it sits close to the InP/CPO materials layer, but it still needs to prove that design activity becomes durable earnings. Top risks: small-cap execution, concentration, and the possibility that the ecosystem matures slower than the narrative suggests.
- `4991.TW` GCS Holdings. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The product roadmap is relevant to SiPh and CPO, but the committee sees a pre-scale component supplier rather than a proven bottleneck. Top risks: delayed volume ramps, customer qualification risk, and weak bargaining power.
- `3105.TW` WIN Semiconductors. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: WIN's compound semiconductor capability is valuable, but the project still reads more like an "AI optionality" thesis than a confirmed photonics supercycle winner. Top risks: continued dependence on legacy RF/mobile exposure and a slower pivot into optical demand.
- `SIVE.ST` Sivers Semiconductors. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: The technology and partner set are real enough to monitor, and the CHIPS-linked R&D money helps, but this is still venture-stage public equity. Top risks: funding, lack of scale, and a long gap between demos and profitable shipments.
- `POET` POET Technologies. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: The platform could matter if wafer-level optical integration works at scale, but the committee would not underwrite it before evidence of repeatable commercial revenue. Top risks: pre-revenue status, cash burn, partner dependence, and timeline slippage.
- `IPGP` IPG Photonics. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: This is a strong photonics company in the generic sense, but it does not fit the AI data center optical thesis tightly enough to stay high on the list. Top risks: industrial and defense demand dominate results, so the stock can miss the supercycle even if the broader photonics narrative is correct.
- `LASR` nLIGHT Inc. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: The committee does not buy this as an AI photonics play; it looks like a defense laser company catching sympathy flows from the optics narrative. Top risks: little direct data center exposure, hype outrunning fundamentals, and a defense cycle that has nothing to do with AI optical interconnects.

## Foundries, Substrates, and Upstream Materials

- `TSEM` Tower Semiconductor. Repo: `BUY`. IC take: Tower is one of the clearest chokepoints in the project because many downstream winners depend on its silicon photonics capacity, and the existing reserved expansion supports the thesis. Top risks: eventual diversification toward `GFS`, `UMC`, or `STM`, customer vertical integration, and dependence on `SOI.PA` at the substrate layer.
- `GFS` GlobalFoundries. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The photonics strategy is credible and the CHIPS-backed U.S. angle matters, but committee conviction is capped because the legacy foundry business still sets the valuation and margins. Top risks: silicon photonics remains too small to move the total company, and the core foundry business stays structurally mediocre.
- `SOI.PA` Soitec SA. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Soitec looks like a real foundational choke point because photonics-grade SOI is difficult to qualify and hard to replace. Top risks: the market may continue valuing the company through weaker mobile demand, and customers could work over time to qualify alternatives or internalize more of the stack.
- `UMC / 2303.TW` United Microelectronics Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: UMC's entry validates the market, but it is still more of an option on future photonics diversification than a present bottleneck. Top risks: late entry, slow customer adoption, and the possibility that Tower remains dominant for longer than expected.
- `STM` STMicroelectronics NV. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: STMicro's platform is credible, but the committee would want harder evidence of customer traction before assigning real strategic value to the SiPh business. Top risks: photonics stays small inside a broad semiconductor company and capacity plans outrun demand.
- `INTC` Intel Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Intel's photonics technology looks impressive, and the CHIPS award helps the capital side, but the corporate situation is messy enough that the committee cannot treat optics as investable on a standalone basis here. Top risks: restructuring, weak execution in the core business, and failure to monetize internal photonics leadership.
- `XFAB.PA` X-FAB Silicon Foundries SE. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The heterogeneous integration angle is differentiated, but the committee views this as an early platform bet rather than a near-term supercycle winner. Top risks: delayed production timing, limited customer proof, and the possibility that larger foundries solve similar problems first.
- `AXTI` AXT Inc. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: The substrate position could be powerful in theory, but the committee would not ignore the China manufacturing and profitability risk just because the market is tight. Top risks: geopolitical disruption, fragile economics, and a niche that may be too small to monetize as bulls expect.
- `5016.T` JX Advanced Metals Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: JX is a cleaner geopolitical version of the InP substrate story than `AXTI`, and that matters, but the market size is still niche and the thesis needs volume growth to justify attention. Top risks: a smaller-than-expected substrate profit pool and limited market appetite for a materials niche inside a broader group.
- `IQE.L` IQE plc. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Strategic value and takeover optionality are real, but the committee does not confuse M&A optionality with a durable public-market thesis. Top risks: weak profitability, strategic review disappointment, and more dilution or restructuring if demand stays uneven.
- `VNP.TO` 5N Plus Inc. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The upstream materials role is genuine, but photonics is only one of several demand vectors, which makes the thesis less direct than the report implies. Top risks: non-photonics end markets dominate the earnings path, and commodity-like pricing overwhelms the purity narrative.
- `4973.T` Japan Pure Chemical. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: This is exactly the kind of obscure supplier a committee wants to investigate, because the niche sounds real and the valuation is not demanding. Top risks: tiny company scale, key-customer exposure, and the possibility that the actual economic importance of plating chemicals is smaller than the narrative suggests.

## Packaging, Test, and Semiconductor Equipment

- `0522.HK` ASMPT Limited. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: AMICRA's assembly role does look bottleneck-like, and this is one of the more plausible underfollowed picks-and-shovels names in the project. Top risks: equipment cyclicality, customer capex pauses, and the fact that optics is still nested inside a much broader packaging-tools company.
- `042700.KS` Hanmi Semiconductor. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The bridge from HBM bonding into CPO is interesting, but the recent share loss risk at SK hynix is exactly the kind of evidence a committee should take seriously. Top risks: moat erosion, CPO adoption happening later than the market hopes, and overreliance on one flagship customer.
- `BESI.AS` BE Semiconductor Industries. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The committee is more constructive here than the repo's middle-of-the-road stance because hybrid bonding and advanced die attach still look like real bottlenecks if CPO and advanced packaging expand. Top risks: the timing of hybrid bonding remains uncertain, and equipment names always suffer if customers pause one generation.
- `KLIC` Kulicke & Soffa Industries. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: KLIC deserves a place in the file, but second-place positioning makes it harder to underwrite than `BESI.AS` or `0522.HK`. Top risks: slower order conversion, competition in thermocompression bonding, and the possibility that SiPh packaging stays too niche.
- `3711.TW` ASE Technology Holding (ASE Group). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: ASE looks like one of the better ways to own the packaging layer because it has scale, customer relevance, and a real path to photonics packaging revenue. Top risks: the photonics piece may remain too small relative to the entire OSAT business, and competition can pressure returns in mature packaging layers.
- `AMKR` Amkor Technology. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The U.S. packaging angle and CHIPS award are useful, but committee preference still goes to `3711.TW` because Amkor looks less differentiated on the photonics layer itself. Top risks: capital intensity, lower margin structure, and the possibility that packaging growth disappoints after the initial hype cycle.
- `FORM` FormFactor Inc. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Keystone gives FormFactor a sensible optical testing adjacency, but the committee wants proof that this becomes a material earnings driver rather than an interesting capability. Top risks: the niche stays small, customer qualification takes longer than expected, and the business remains tied to broader semiconductor test cycles.
- `AEHR` Aehr Test Systems. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: The wafer-level burn-in niche is real, but the valuation and earnings quality are not good enough for the committee to underwrite it today. Top risks: lumpiness, margin pressure, and dependence on a very small set of customers and programs.
- `6857.T` Advantest Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Advantest is one of the best businesses in the whole file, even if photonics is only part of the story, because it owns a mission-critical test layer with strong pricing power. Top risks: the current AI test boom normalizes, and photonics remains too small to matter versus the broader semiconductor cycle.
- `TER` Teradyne Inc. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Teradyne benefits from the same structural test logic as Advantest, and Quantifi Photonics gives it a specific angle the committee likes. Top risks: still second to Advantest in key pockets, and test orders can whip around with customer capex timing.
- `6146.T` Disco Corporation. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Disco is a high-quality, underappreciated toll collector because every advanced chip has to be diced and ground before shipment, including photonic ICs. Top risks: valuation and semicap cyclicality, though the underlying moat looks real.
- `VECO` Veeco Instruments. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The MOCVD link is valid, but the committee still sees a cyclical equipment supplier rather than a narrow photonics bottleneck. Top risks: end-market volatility, merger or integration noise, and optoelectronics remaining too small to offset other exposure.
- `AIXA.DE` Aixtron SE. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Aixtron may be the cleaner way to own MOCVD than Veeco because the compound-semiconductor franchise is clearer, but the thesis still depends on optoelectronics outgrowing weaker SiC demand. Top risks: cyclical drag from other end markets and the possibility that the AI optics piece is overestimated.
- `AMAT` Applied Materials. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Applied Materials is absolutely relevant as an enabling tool vendor, and the CHIPS-linked R&D award helps, but the company is simply too broad to be a targeted photonics idea. Top risks: photonics never becomes material to valuation and the market continues to price AMAT mainly on logic/memory equipment cycles.
- `LRCX` Lam Research. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Lam matters technically, but the committee would classify it as an important context name rather than a differentiated investment within this theme. Top risks: the photonics use case stays tiny and normal semicap cyclicality dominates.
- `ASML` ASML Holding NV. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Great company, obvious tool monopoly, wrong level of specificity for this project. Top risks: very little of the photonics upside is incremental to what investors already own through the broader lithography thesis.
- `AEIS` Advanced Energy Industries. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The dual exposure to fabs and data centers is attractive, but the committee does not see a unique photonics moat. Top risks: AI demand proves cyclical, and power-supply economics remain more competitive than the narrative implies.
- `MKSI` MKS Instruments. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: MKS belongs in the ecosystem, but the repo materials do not show a reason it should earn a supercycle multiple. Top risks: generic semicap cyclicality and limited differentiation inside this theme.
- `JEN.DE` Jenoptik AG. Repo: `AVOID`. IC take: Jenoptik has some relevant photonics and semicap exposure, but the committee agrees it is too indirect and too cyclical for this specific mandate. Top risks: weak core end markets and no clear AI optical choke point.
- `VIAV` VIAVI Solutions. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: VIAVI is a sensible picks-and-shovels name because optical test is essential and valuation looks saner than many optics peers. Top risks: spending pauses after qualification cycles and the market continuing to view the company as a slow-growth instrument vendor.

## Fiber, Cabling, and Optical Infrastructure

- `GLW` Corning Incorporated. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Corning is a real infrastructure beneficiary and the CHIPS support is a positive signal, but the committee would still want a pullback because the market already understands the AI fiber story. Top risks: campus deployment pauses, pricing competition, and the optical segment becoming less supply constrained.
- `5803.T` Fujikura Ltd. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The demand looks real, but this also looks like a momentum-stock version of the fiber thesis with a lot of good news already reflected. Top risks: digestion after rapid buildouts and over-earning on current tight supply.
- `600487.SS` Hengtong Optic-Electric. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: The fiber plus packaging angle is more interesting than a plain cable name, but committee enthusiasm is capped by the China listing and infrastructure cyclicality. Top risks: pricing pressure, project timing swings, and policy risk.
- `6869.HK / 601869.SH` YOFC (Yangtze Optical Fibre and Cable). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: YOFC is an understandable national-champion fiber idea, but fiber is not the narrowest chokepoint in AI optics and the committee would demand a discount for China exposure. Top risks: fiber oversupply, policy friction, and hollow-core fiber remaining more promise than profit.
- `STLTECH.NS` Sterlite Technologies Ltd (STL). Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: STL is a reasonable way to express non-U.S./non-China fiber demand, but the business looks cyclical and project-driven rather than moat-like. Top risks: execution, delayed customer deployments, and weaker pricing if industry supply catches up.
- `PRY.MI` Prysmian SpA. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Hollow-core fiber is strategically interesting, but the committee treats it as a long-duration option, not a present chokepoint. Top risks: adoption comes much later than expected and the stock keeps trading on broader cable and power themes.
- `HUBN.SW` Huber+Suhner AG. Repo: `HOLD`. IC take: Polatis gives Huber+Suhner a real optical circuit switching angle, and that is worth watching because it could become more important in large GPU fabrics. Top risks: OCS adoption stays slower than the projections suggest, or hyperscalers decide to internalize more of the functionality.

## Context Name

- `NVDA` NVIDIA Corporation. Repo: `Context only / not scored`. IC take: NVIDIA is the architectural driver and customer anchor for a large part of this universe, so it matters for channel reading and supplier validation, but it is too broad and too obvious to be the point of this project.
