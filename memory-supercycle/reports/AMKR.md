# AMKR - Amkor Technology
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 74/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Qualified Supplier with Leverage

### Thesis
Amkor is a worthwhile memory-supercycle holding because advanced packaging and test capacity matter more as AI systems get denser and more memory-heavy. The company is the largest U.S.-headquartered OSAT, its advanced products already dominate revenue, and the Arizona buildout gives it strategic optionality in the U.S. AI supply chain. The reason it does not rank higher is simple: margins are still much thinner than the cleaner choke-point names.

### Value Chain Position
Amkor sits in outsourced packaging and test:
- advanced packaging
- wafer-level processing
- system-in-package and related test services

For AI and high-memory systems, this layer becomes increasingly important as heterogeneous integration and geographic supply-chain resilience matter more.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** substrates, leadframes, packaging materials, test equipment, and high-capex advanced-packaging lines.

**Downstream:** major semiconductor firms, foundries, OEMs, and increasingly AI and data-center customers.

**Competitors:** ASE, JCET, TSMC advanced packaging, and other OSAT players.

**Displacement risk:** customers insource more packaging, or Arizona monetization takes longer than expected.

### Moat Analysis
Amkor's moat is scale, footprint, and strategic positioning:
- it calls itself the world's largest U.S.-headquartered OSAT
- in 2024 it set a record for Advanced SiP revenue and generated $5.18B of advanced-products sales
- in October 2025 it expanded the Arizona advanced packaging project to $7B, with Apple and NVIDIA called out as key customers
- the Arizona campus is positioned as the first U.S.-based high-volume advanced packaging facility

This makes Amkor more strategic than its margin profile alone suggests.

### Catalysts
1. Advanced products already represent the large majority of revenue.
2. Q2 2025 showed double-digit growth across all end markets.
3. The Arizona expansion increases Amkor's relevance to U.S. AI and packaging policy.
4. Advanced packaging demand should remain structurally strong even if consumer semis wobble.

### Risks
1. Gross margins are still modest at 12.0%.
2. The Arizona project will require time and capital before revenue arrives.
3. Top ten customers still accounted for 72% of Q2 2025 sales.
4. OSAT is structurally a tougher business than pure tool or probe bottlenecks.

### Verdict: HOLD
Amkor belongs in the project, especially for investors who want advanced-packaging exposure with U.S. optionality. But it is not as clean or profitable as the better choke-point names.

### Price Target: $55
That target assumes advanced-products mix continues to rise and the market gives more credit to Amkor's strategic packaging position, while still discounting its lower structural margins.
