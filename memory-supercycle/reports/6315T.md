# 6315.T - TOWA
## Date: 2026-03-24
## Score: 75/100
## Memory Chokepoint Tier: Qualified Supplier with Leverage

### Thesis
TOWA is an undercovered back-end equipment name that deserves a place in the memory-supercycle basket. It is not a current HBM leader like Hanmi or ASMPT, but it has two valuable traits: strong current order momentum tied to AI and memory, and a credible roadmap into next-generation HBM4 packaging. That makes it a less obvious, but potentially attractive, way to own the packaging layer.

### Value Chain Position
TOWA sits in the back-end equipment layer:
- compression molding equipment
- singulation equipment
- advanced packaging process technology for future HBM4 and panel-level packaging

The company matters because packaging and singulation capacity can become the bottleneck after wafers are made.

### Supply Chain Map
**Upstream:** precision machinery, process modules, service and spare-part support.

**Downstream:** OSATs, memory-related packaging customers, and advanced-packaging lines in Asia.

**Competitors:** other back-end equipment makers, including compression and molding specialists.

**Displacement risk:** HBM4 roadmap slips, or customers favor other packaging methods.

### Moat Analysis
TOWA's moat is narrower than the top HBM bottlenecks, but still real:
- in February 2026 management said memory-related orders remained firm, mainly from AI and data-center applications
- it said compression-equipment orders in 3Q FY2025 hit a record quarterly high
- on March 21, 2025 TOWA announced a next-generation HBM4 packaging technology using compression molding and laser-assisted direct bonding, targeting panel-level applications by 2027
- the company is a leading manufacturer in the back-end process, not a generic semi-cap name

### Catalysts
1. Memory and AI-related orders remain firm.
2. Compression orders have recently been increasing, which should improve mix and margins.
3. HBM4 panel-level packaging gives the stock longer-term optionality if customers adopt it.
4. The stock is well off its high despite a still-firm memory order environment.

### Risks
1. TOWA is not a pure HBM tool leader today.
2. Revenue recognition can slip when customer capex timing moves.
3. Product mix swings hit profitability quickly.
4. A lot of the HBM4 upside is roadmap optionality, not current earnings.

### Verdict: BUY
TOWA is a second-line packaging name rather than the core bottleneck, but it is underfollowed, profitable, balance-sheet strong, and tied to a real memory-order recovery.

### Price Target: JPY 3,000
That target assumes ongoing memory-order strength and better monetization of compression-equipment demand, without pricing in full success of its HBM4 packaging roadmap.
