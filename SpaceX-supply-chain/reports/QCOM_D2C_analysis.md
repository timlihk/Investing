# Qualcomm (QCOM) — X105 NR-NTN Modem & Starlink D2C Analysis
## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 55/100 (as SpaceX/D2C play)
## Rating: HOLD — NTN is a supporting catalyst, not the primary thesis

## The X105 Modem
- **First Release 19-capable modem** with native NR-NTN (satellite) connectivity
- TSMC 3nm (est.), integrated modem-RF, 14.8 Gbps DL / 4.2 Gbps UL (terrestrial)
- NR-NTN is a **3GPP open standard** — not Starlink-exclusive. Any compliant satellite constellation works
- First devices late 2026 / mass production 2027 (Samsung Galaxy S27, Xiaomi 17, OnePlus 14)

## Competitive Position

| | Qualcomm X105 | MediaTek | Samsung LSI |
|---|---|---|---|
| NR-NTN modem | **Shipping 2026** | 12-18 months behind | Behind |
| 3GPP Release | **Rel. 19** | Rel. 18 (partial) | Rel. 17/18 |
| RF Front-End | **Integrated (own)** | Third-party | Limited |
| Premium market share | 75-80% | 15-20% | ~5% |

**12-18 month structural lead**, but NTN is an open standard — MediaTek will close the gap.

## The Real Value: Defense, Not Offense
NTN's **defensive value** (protecting $26.5B handset design wins) may be worth more than its incremental revenue:
- If QCOM has NTN and MediaTek doesn't, Samsung/Xiaomi/OPPO **cannot switch to MediaTek**
- Apple wild card: if Apple's in-house modem lacks NTN, creates extended QTL licensing opportunity

## Investment Verdict

**NTN alone adds ~10-14% to QCOM's enterprise value** ($15-25B on base case). At $165 / ~14x fwd P/E, QCOM is reasonable but NTN alone doesn't make it a SpaceX play.

**Better framing:** QCOM is a premium semiconductor franchise that happens to be first-to-market on the most important new wireless feature since 5G. NTN is one of several growth vectors (auto, PC/AI, IoT, XR).

**Score 55/100 in SpaceX framework** — confirmed involvement, large TAM, but open standard erodes moat over time, and NTN is 4-6% of future revenue, not transformative for a $183B company.

**Watch for:** First X105 phones (late 2026), Starlink Mobile commercial launch pricing, Apple modem NTN roadmap, MediaTek NTN timeline.
