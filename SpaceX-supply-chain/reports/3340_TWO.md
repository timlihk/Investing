## Company

Shenmao Technology Inc. -- Taiwan-based solder materials manufacturer. Products include solder paste, solder wire, solder bar, BGA solder balls, flux, and semiconductor packaging materials. Founded 1973, headquartered in Taoyuan, ~207 employees.

## SpaceX Relationship

**CONFIRMED** -- Solder materials supplier for Starlink PCB assembly. SpaceX has requested Shenmao relocate production to Vietnam, resulting in a $5M facility investment. This is part of SpaceX's broader Vietnam manufacturing hub strategy (alongside WNC, Chin-Poon, and others).

The fact that SpaceX asked Shenmao to move to Vietnam demonstrates SpaceX leverage over Shenmao, not the reverse. SpaceX dictates supply chain geography.

## Why AVOID

1. **Commodity solder materials** -- solder paste, wire, and balls are available from multiple global suppliers with equivalent performance.
2. **SpaceX has the leverage** -- forcing Shenmao to invest $5M in Vietnam relocation shows who has bargaining power.
3. **Margin compression** -- FY2025 earnings flat despite 29% revenue growth. Metal input costs eating margins.
4. **Small-cap illiquidity** -- TWD 14.7B market cap with limited analyst coverage.
5. **Elevated valuation** -- 35.5x P/E for a commodity materials company with flat earnings is expensive.

## Bull Case

- Vietnam facility positions Shenmao for broader Southeast Asia electronics supply chain demand.
- 5-10% SpaceX exposure is more meaningful here than at larger companies.
- Revenue growth remains strong at 29% YoY.
- If Starlink terminal volumes scale to 5-10M/yr, solder revenue could double.

## Verdict

**AVOID.** Confirmed and slightly more SpaceX-exposed (5-10%) than typical BOM suppliers, but solder is a commodity with no pricing power. The Vietnam facility investment is a cost, not a moat. Flat earnings at 35x P/E is unattractive.
