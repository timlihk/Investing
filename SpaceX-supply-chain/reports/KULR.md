# KULR - KULR Technology Group

## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 22/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Commodity / Indirect

### Thesis
KULR Technology is a micro-cap ($127M) battery safety and thermal management company that was "selected by one of the world's largest privately held space exploration companies" (thinly veiled SpaceX reference) for enhanced battery safety testing using the KULR ONE Design Solutions platform. The company is also launching a KULR ONE Space (K1S) battery on a SpaceX rideshare mission in 2026, targeting the $6.35B space battery market. However, KULR is a pre-profit company doing ~$17M TTM revenue with -$22M net losses, a history of dilution (reverse split in June 2025), and the SpaceX "relationship" is a battery testing service contract -- not embedded supply chain hardware.

### SpaceX Relationship: PROBABLE (Service Contract, Not Hardware Supply)
- KULR announced selection by "one of the world's largest privately held space exploration companies" for battery safety testing -- almost certainly SpaceX
- This is a service/testing contract, not an embedded hardware supply relationship
- SpaceX rideshare mission planned for K1S battery in 2026 (KULR is the *customer*, not the supplier)
- NASA JSC 20793 compliance is real but does not create lock-in
- **Estimate: SpaceX contract value likely <$1M; KULR is providing testing services, not mission-critical hardware**

### Value Chain Position
KULR operates at the TESTING/VALIDATION tier -- providing battery safety analysis and thermal management solutions.
- **Battery Safety Testing**: Using KULR ONE platform for cell-level safety characterization
- **KULR ONE Space Battery**: Developing COTS space battery; received $6.7M Texas Space Commission grant
- **Thermal Management**: Carbon fiber thermal interface materials for electronics cooling

### Verdict: AVOID
KULR has a real but immaterial SpaceX connection -- providing battery safety testing services, not mission-critical flight hardware. The company is pre-profit, micro-cap, and has a dilution-heavy history. Revenue growth is impressive in percentage terms but from a tiny base (~$17M).
