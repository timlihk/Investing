# Starshield Military Supply Chain Analysis
## Date: 2026-03-29

## Key Finding: Starshield Uses Same Starlink Bus = Same Suppliers Win

Starshield is SpaceX's military/government satellite program. **$7-10B in known contracts** over 5-7 years (NRO $1.8B, MILNET ~480 sats, Golden Dome ~600 sats). But the critical insight for supply chain investors:

**Starshield does NOT require rad-hard components.** SpaceX uses the same COTS-with-redundancy philosophy for both commercial Starlink and military Starshield. The satellite bus is Starlink-derived. ITAR-specific additions are at the PAYLOAD and ENCRYPTION level only.

This means: **STM, Filtronic, and other confirmed Starlink suppliers also supply Starshield automatically.**

## ITAR/AUKUS — Who Wins, Who Loses

### AUKUS Exemption = Game-Changer for Filtronic

On Sept 1, 2024, the **AUKUS ITAR exemption** took effect. Final rule published Dec 30, 2025. This removes licensing requirements for **70-80% of commercial defense trade** between US, UK, and Australia.

**Filtronic (UK) can supply Starshield without individual export licenses.** This is a massive competitive advantage over non-Five Eyes suppliers.

### Impact by Supplier

| Supplier | Country | ITAR Status | Starshield Impact |
|----------|---------|------------|-------------------|
| **Filtronic** | UK | **AUKUS exempt** | **STRONG BENEFICIARY** — can supply both commercial + military programs |
| **STM** | France | NATO ally, not AUKUS | **MODERATE RISK** — satellite bus components likely OK, but encryption/payload modules need US sourcing |
| **Taiwan names** (Compeq, UMT, Chin-Poon) | Taiwan | **NOT AUKUS** | **HIGHEST RISK** — face geopolitical headwinds + no AUKUS exemption for defense work |
| **Microchip Technology** | US | **ITAR compliant** | MODERATE BENEFICIARY — benefits from broader SDA/Golden Dome ecosystem, not Starshield directly (SpaceX still uses COTS) |

## Supplier-Level Analysis

### Filtronic — BIGGEST WINNER
- AUKUS exemption enables defense supply without friction
- Already winning UK defense contracts (DASA, Leonardo, QinetiQ, BAE Maritime)
- If Starshield gateways use same E-band SSPAs as Starlink (likely), Filtronic supplies both
- **Starshield strengthens the FLTCF investment thesis**

### STM — Neutral to Moderate Risk
- Satellite bus is Starlink-derived → STM chips flow into Starshield automatically
- France is NATO ally with existing defense trade agreements
- Risk: if Pentagon mandates US-only sourcing for entire satellite (unlikely given SpaceX's COTS model)
- Assessment: STM likely continues supplying base platform. Only encryption/payload modules use ITAR-specific sourcing

### Microchip (MCHP) — Broader Ecosystem Beneficiary
- Rad-hard chips not needed for SpaceX's COTS Starshield satellites
- BUT benefits from **non-SpaceX military satellite programs** (SDA Tranches to L3Harris, Lockheed, Northrop, Rocket Lab — these primes DO use rad-hard)
- $6B+ in SDA Tranche contracts to traditional primes = Microchip's real TAM

### Taiwan Suppliers — Most at Risk
- ITAR restrictions apply to any Starshield-adjacent work
- No AUKUS exemption
- SpaceX already pushing Taiwan suppliers to Vietnam/Thailand for geopolitical de-risking
- For COMMERCIAL Starlink: no restriction. For MILITARY Starshield: significant ITAR friction

## Bottom Line

Starshield is **incremental to the Starlink investment thesis, not transformative.** The key insight is that the same suppliers win because Starshield uses the same satellite bus. Filtronic benefits most due to AUKUS exemption. The real military satellite money ($50B+) flows through traditional primes (L3Harris, Lockheed, Northrop) where Microchip/rad-hard suppliers have more relevance than in SpaceX's COTS approach.
