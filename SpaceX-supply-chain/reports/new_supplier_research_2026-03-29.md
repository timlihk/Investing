## TIER 1 — Confirmed/Probable with Investment Merit

### 1. OUT1V.HE / OUTFF — Outokumpu Oyj
**Evidence: Probable | Chokepoint: 2/5 | Stainless steel for Starship**

- **What**: Europe's largest stainless steel producer; US operations at Calvert, AL
- **Evidence**: Multiple sources identify Outokumpu as a stainless steel supplier for SpaceX Starship (same 30X alloy used in Tesla Cybertruck). TeslaNorth confirmed "Tesla Taps SpaceX Supplier for Cybertruck Stainless Steel" referencing Outokumpu. Also listed alongside ArcelorMittal and NSSC as Starship steel providers
- **Why it matters**: Starship uses ~200+ tonnes of stainless steel per vehicle. At planned launch cadence (12-24 Starships/year), this is meaningful volume
- **Chokepoint weakness**: Commodity stainless steel with many alternatives (STLD already covered, plus ArcelorMittal, NSSC). Zero switching cost. SpaceX's 30X alloy can be rolled by multiple mills
- **Financials**: Revenue EUR 5.9B (2025), market cap ~$2.1B, cyclically depressed
- **Verdict**: LOW PRIORITY. Commodity supplier with no lock-in. SpaceX demand is rounding error. Already covered STLD for this angle. SKIP unless looking for cheap European steel cyclical play

### 2. 6674.T — GS Yuasa Corporation
**Evidence: Speculated | Chokepoint: 3/5 | Satellite battery cells**

- **What**: World leader in lithium-ion cells for space (250+ satellites, 5.0+ MWh flown). Tokyo-listed, ~JPY 400B market cap
- **Evidence**: GS Yuasa is THE dominant space-qualified Li-ion cell manufacturer globally. Their LSE-series cells power >250 space vehicles. EaglePicher (private, major US satellite battery maker) uses GS Yuasa cells as inputs. However, NO confirmed direct SpaceX relationship. SpaceX Starlink uses silicon solar panels + Li-ion batteries but the specific cell supplier is unknown publicly
- **Why it matters**: Starlink constellation of 12,000+ satellites, each with battery packs. If GS Yuasa supplies even a fraction, it's meaningful. But SpaceX may use commercial/automotive-grade cells (Tesla 2170s?) rather than space-grade
- **Chokepoint assessment**: If space-qualified: high chokepoint (few alternatives). If commercial-grade: zero chokepoint (many cell suppliers)
- **Estimated chokepoint scores**: Breakage 3, Alternatives 3, Qualification 3, COGS 2, Capacity 3 = 14/25 IF confirmed
- **Verdict**: RESEARCH FURTHER. Key question: does SpaceX use space-grade or commercial Li-ion cells on Starlink? If commercial (likely given cost focus), skip. If space-grade, GS Yuasa is the obvious supplier

**Evidence: Probable | Chokepoint: 2/5 | Helium for launch operations**

## TIER 2 — Launch Consumables & Starship-Specific

### 6. TEA-TEB Igniter Fluid
**Suppliers: NO PUBLIC PURE-PLAY**

- TEA-TEB (triethylaluminium-triethylborane) is the pyrophoric igniter fluid for Falcon 9 Merlin engines
- Triethylaluminium manufacturers: Albemarle (ALB), Lanxess, Nouryon (private). Triethylborane: smaller specialty chem suppliers
- **Key finding**: No public company has meaningful revenue from TEA-TEB. Total global market is tiny (specialty pyrophoric chemical). Albemarle makes organometallics but it's <1% of their lithium-dominated business
- **Starship note**: Raptor engines use torch igniters (electric), NOT TEA-TEB. So this is Falcon 9 only, with declining relevance as Starship ramps
- **Verdict**: NO INVESTABLE ANGLE. Commodity specialty chemical with no public pure-play

### 7. RP-1 Rocket Kerosene
**Suppliers: Haltermann Carless (PRIVATE)**

- Haltermann Carless (subsidiary of H&R GmbH, Germany) is the primary known RP-1 manufacturer
- H&R GmbH trades as 2HRA.DE on Frankfurt but is a tiny specialty refiner
- **Starship note**: Starship uses methane (LNG), NOT RP-1. Falcon 9 uses RP-1, but Falcon 9 is being phased out over time
- **Verdict**: DECLINING RELEVANCE. RP-1 demand shrinks as Starship replaces Falcon 9. No investable angle

### 8. 2HRA.DE — H&R GmbH (Haltermann Carless parent)
**Evidence: Probable (for RP-1) | Chokepoint: 2/5**

- Frankfurt-listed specialty refiner, market cap ~EUR 500M
- Haltermann Solutions division produces RP-1 to MIL-SPEC
- **Verdict**: SKIP. Declining relevance (Starship = methane) and too small/illiquid for most investors

## TIER 3 — Satellite Bus Components

### 9. Reaction Wheels / Star Trackers / Attitude Control
**SpaceX builds ALL of these in-house**

- Research confirms: SpaceX builds its own reaction wheels ("four reaction wheels provide agile attitude control for Starlink vehicles, with a hot-spare configuration")
- Star trackers: SpaceX builds in-house
- Hall-effect thrusters: SpaceX builds in-house (switched from krypton V1 to argon V2+)
- Solar panels: SpaceX uses silicon (NOT GaAs), manufactures in-house at scale
- **Key insight**: SpaceX's extreme vertical integration means there are NO public suppliers for core satellite bus components. Blue Canyon Technologies (RTX subsidiary) supplies reaction wheels to Kuiper (Amazon), NOT Starlink. Rocket Lab (RKLB) sells to other constellations, NOT SpaceX
- **Verdict**: DEAD END. SpaceX's vertical integration in satellite subsystems is nearly complete. No investable supplier angle

### 10. Argon Gas (V2+ Satellite Propellant)
**Suppliers: Linde (LIN), Air Products (APD), Air Liquide (AI.PA) — already covered**

- Starlink V2 satellites switched from krypton to argon for Hall-effect thrusters
- Argon costs ~$10 per satellite — utterly negligible
- Supplied by industrial gas majors (already in universe)
- **Verdict**: NO NEW ANGLE. Already covered via LIN/APD/AI.PA

## TIER 4 — Ground Station Infrastructure (Largely Opaque)

### 11. Ground Station Networking / Fiber / Timing
**NO CONFIRMED PUBLIC SUPPLIERS FOUND**

- SpaceX operates 170+ ground stations ("gateways") with E-band backhaul (70-80 GHz)
- RF backhaul: Filtronic (FLTCF) supplies E-band SSPAs — already covered
- Fiber interconnect: SpaceX connects gateways to internet backbone via fiber but specific transceiver/switch suppliers are not publicly disclosed
- Precision timing: Microchip (MCHP, already covered) is the dominant GPS-disciplined oscillator maker, but no confirmed SpaceX ground station contract
- Networking equipment: Likely commodity Ethernet switches (Arista, Cisco, or white-box). No confirmed supplier
- **Key finding**: SpaceX ground stations are largely a black box. The only confirmed public supplier is Filtronic for E-band amplifiers. Everything else (switches, routers, timing, power) is either commodity or not disclosed
- **Verdict**: FILTRONIC REMAINS THE ONLY CONFIRMED GROUND STATION PLAY. No new names found

### The Overarching Insight:
SpaceX's ~85% vertical integration rate means the investable supply chain is concentrated in:
1. **Semiconductors** (STM, AMD, MPWR — cannot easily in-house chip fabs)
2. **Specialty RF** (Filtronic — E-band GaN amplifiers)
3. **Specialty alloys** (ATI, CRS, 347700.KQ — metallurgy is hard)
4. **Industrial gases** (LIN, AI.PA — physics-constrained)
5. **PCB/packaging** (Taiwan supply chain — being in-housed over 2026-2027)

The PCB/packaging category is under active threat from SpaceX's Bastrop TX factory (FOPLP by Q3 2026, PCB already operational). Semiconductor and specialty materials remain the most durable chokepoints.
