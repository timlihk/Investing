# MCHP — Microchip Technology
## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 40/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Oligopoly with Moat

### Thesis
Microchip holds 19% of the space-grade semiconductor market (Databeans), 3x larger than any competitor. Its rad-hard MCUs, FPGAs (RT PolarFire), and power converters are used across military, GEO, MEO, and LEO satellite programs. Lockheed Martin partnered with Microchip in 2025 to integrate rad-hard semiconductors into satellite systems. However, no confirmed direct SpaceX supplier relationship exists in public filings. SpaceX famously uses commercial-grade (not rad-hard) components on Starlink satellites where possible, limiting Microchip's Starlink relevance. Better positioned for government/Starshield and traditional defense satellite programs.

### SpaceX Relationship: HIGHLY PROBABLE (indirect)
- **No confirmed direct SpaceX supplier relationship** in public filings or IR
- 19% space-grade semiconductor market share makes indirect exposure very likely
- Lockheed Martin partnership (2025) for rad-hard satellite integration
- Rad-hard MCUs (ATmegaS128, SAMV71Q21RT), FPGAs (RT PolarFire), power converters used across satellite industry
- SpaceX Starshield (military variant) more likely to use space-grade parts than commercial Starlink
- Aerospace & defense grew to 17-18% of revenue in FY2025 (up from 11% prior year)

### Risks
- **#1: SpaceX COTS philosophy** — Starlink uses commercial-grade parts, limiting rad-hard TAM to Starshield/Dragon only
- **#2: Unconfirmed relationship** — no public filing or IR mention of SpaceX
- **#3: Cyclical trough** — revenue down 42%, margin compression, elevated debt
- **#4: Vertical integration** — SpaceX could develop in-house rad-hard solutions for Starshield
- **#5: Competition** — BAE Systems, Renesas, Infineon all competing in rad-hard space

### Verdict: HOLD
Strong space-grade semiconductor franchise (19% market share) with genuine qualification moats, but no confirmed SpaceX direct relationship. Better framed as a defense/space semiconductor play broadly, with Starshield optionality. The cyclical trough creates a compelling entry if you believe in MCU/FPGA recovery, but the SpaceX thesis specifically is unconfirmed.

**One-liner:** Dominant space-grade chipmaker with qualification moats, but SpaceX uses commercial-grade parts on Starlink — thesis is Starshield optionality, not confirmed supply.
