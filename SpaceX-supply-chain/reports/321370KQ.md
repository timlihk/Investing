---

## Thesis

Sensorview is a Korean mmWave RF solutions company that has supplied prototype RF cables to SpaceX for Starlink ground equipment and holds registered vendor status. The company also supplies test/measurement cables to AST SpaceMobile. However, the SpaceX relationship is pre-revenue for satellite-grade products, unverified for mass production, and dwarfed by the company's pivot toward Korean defense (M-SAM, LAMD). This is a thematic play on space/defense buzzwords, not a genuine SpaceX chokepoint.

## SpaceX Relationship

**Status: WEAK -- Vendor registered, prototype stage only. No confirmed mass-production revenue.**

| Claim | Evidence | Confidence |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Registered SpaceX vendor | Company disclosure; supplied ground equipment RF cables since ~2018 | Medium |
| Starlink satellite RF cable prototypes | Korean press reports; company says "new product development stage" with ongoing performance testing | Low-Medium |
| Radar antenna supplier to SpaceX | Seoul Economic Daily report; **NOT independently verified** | Low |
| Mass production orders from SpaceX | **None disclosed** | None |

The original thesis (per Seoul Economic Daily) was that Sensorview supplies "radar antennas" to SpaceX. In reality, the company supplied high-frequency RF cables for SpaceX ground equipment starting around 2018 and has been in discussions to supply satellite-grade RF cables for Starlink. As of March 2026, there is no public disclosure of mass-production orders or meaningful revenue from SpaceX.

**AST SpaceMobile relationship:** In January 2025, Sensorview announced it would supply test/measurement cables to AST SpaceMobile. This is a real but small contract -- AST is itself a pre-revenue company with uncertain satellite deployment timelines.

## Chokepoint Assessment

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **What does Sensorview supply SpaceX?** | Prototype RF cables for ground equipment; satellite-grade cables in testing. NOT radar antennas as initially claimed. |
| **Is it a chokepoint?** | **No.** RF cables are a commoditized category with many qualified suppliers globally (Times Microwave, Huber+Suhner, Carlisle, etc.) |
| **Alternatives for SpaceX** | Abundant. US-based RF cable makers (Times Microwave, Pasternack, Carlisle Interconnect) are more likely long-term suppliers for a US defense/space prime. |
| **SpaceX in-housing risk** | High. SpaceX vertically integrates aggressively. RF cables are not complex enough to warrant external dependency. |
| **Revenue exposure to SpaceX** | Effectively zero confirmed mass-production revenue. Prototype/testing stage only. |
| **Switching cost** | Low. RF cables are tested to spec; once qualified, any supplier meeting spec can substitute. |

---

## Risk Factors

1. **No confirmed SpaceX production revenue** -- The entire SpaceX thesis is based on vendor registration and prototype supply. No mass-production orders have been disclosed.
2. **Chronic losses** -- The company has never been profitable. Even the bullish FY2026E forecast only targets breakeven.
3. **KOSDAQ micro-cap volatility** -- KRW 128B market cap with extreme retail-driven swings. Not suitable for institutional capital.
4. **Thematic momentum trap** -- Stock surged 5x on SpaceX IPO hype. The narrative far exceeds the fundamental reality.
5. **Defense execution risk** -- The FY2026 revenue surge depends on M-SAM/LAMD mass production deliveries that have not yet been fully realized.
6. **Dilution risk** -- Pre-profit companies on KOSDAQ frequently raise capital via CBs, warrants, or secondary offerings.
7. **Currency/access risk** -- KOSDAQ-listed, KRW-denominated, limited foreign investor access and coverage.
8. **SpaceX in-housing** -- SpaceX is known for vertical integration. Even if Sensorview wins initial orders, SpaceX may bring production in-house.
9. **AST SpaceMobile counterparty risk** -- AST itself is a speculative, pre-revenue satellite company. Supply contracts to AST carry counterparty risk.

## Verdict

**AVOID as a SpaceX supply chain investment.** The SpaceX connection is tenuous -- vendor-registered for ground equipment cables at prototype stage with no mass-production revenue. The real story is Korean defense (M-SAM, LAMD), which is a different thesis entirely. At a KRW 128B market cap for a money-losing KOSDAQ micro-cap, the risk/reward is unattractive. The stock has already had its speculative run on SpaceX IPO hype.

If interested in the Korean defense angle specifically, wait for confirmed profitability in H2 2026 and a price closer to KRW 1,500-2,000.

**One-liner:** SpaceX vendor-registered for prototype RF cables only -- not a chokepoint; real story is Korean defense, which is already priced in after a 5x speculative run.
