# ACX.MC - Acerinox (via Haynes International)
## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 32/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Competitive with Differentiation

### Thesis
Acerinox acquired Haynes International for $799M (Nov 2024), gaining the world's leading brand in high-temperature nickel superalloys (HASTELLOY, HAYNES). Haynes alloys have a storied history in rocket engines (Space Shuttle used 47 Haynes 188 parts + Hastelloy B/C-22 in fuel bellows). SpaceX likely uses Haynes-type superalloys in Raptor components, but the relationship is unconfirmed and SpaceX develops proprietary alloys (SX300/SX500) in-house. Acerinox itself is primarily a stainless steel commodity producer -- the high-performance alloys division is only ~25% of EBITDA.

### SpaceX Relationship: SPECULATED (Moderate Probability)
- No confirmed SpaceX supply contract in any filing or press release
- Haynes alloys have historical rocket engine use (Shuttle, general aerospace)
- SpaceX Raptor uses Inconel-family superalloys but develops proprietary variants in-house
- Haynes is one of ~3-4 Western producers of aerospace-grade Ni superalloy feedstock (alongside ATI, CRS/Special Metals, VDM)
- **Estimate: SpaceX-specific revenue likely <0.5% of Acerinox total, <2% of Haynes division**

### Value Chain Position
Acerinox sits at MIDSTREAM -- melting and fabricating specialty alloys.
- **Stainless Steel**: ~75% of revenue. Commodity flat/long products (NAS in Kentucky, Acerinox Europa in Spain, Columbus in South Africa, Bahru in Malaysia)
- **High-Performance Alloys (Haynes)**: ~25% of EBITDA. HASTELLOY (corrosion-resistant) and HAYNES (high-temp) alloys for aerospace, chemical processing, energy

### Verdict: AVOID
Haynes International is a quality asset and Acerinox got it at a reasonable price. But the SpaceX connection is speculative -- Haynes makes generic aerospace superalloys, not purpose-built SpaceX parts. The stainless steel business (~75% of the company) is pure commodity cyclical. At EUR 12.93, the stock may be interesting as a metals cycle recovery play, but not as a SpaceX supply chain investment.

**One-liner:** Haynes makes world-class superalloys, but Acerinox is 75% commodity stainless -- buying it for SpaceX is like buying a mall for one boutique.
