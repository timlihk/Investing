## TIER 1: Confirmed or Near-Confirmed SpaceX Relationship

### 005930.KS -- Samsung Electronics (삼성전자)
**What they supply:** AI-powered Exynos satellite modem chip for Starlink D2D (direct-to-device) connectivity, fabricated on Samsung 3nm GAA process.
**Evidence:** Multiple credible sources (KED Global Oct 2025, Tom's Hardware, Mobile World Live) report Samsung System LSI division developing Exynos NTN modem with NPU for SpaceX. Senior Samsung semiconductor exec met SpaceX officials to share technical details. Galaxy S26 ships with Exynos 6000 featuring native NTN modem. Beta D2D service targeting 2026, full 6G NTN by 2030.
**Chokepoint assessment:** LOW. Samsung is one of several modem players (Qualcomm X105 already confirmed). This is a development-stage chip, not yet shipping at scale. Also, Samsung at $280B+ market cap makes SpaceX revenue immaterial (<0.01%).
**Investment thesis:** AVOID for SpaceX thesis. Too diversified. Qualcomm (already covered) has confirmed 12-18mo lead with X105. Samsung's Exynos modem is a competitive response, not a chokepoint.
**Verdict:** AVOID -- interesting technically but not investable on SpaceX thesis alone.

### 274090.KQ -- Kencoa Aerospace (켄코아에어로스페이스)
**What they supply:** Special materials and alloys for SpaceX rockets via US subsidiary California Metal & Supply Inc.
**Evidence:** Kencoa won SpaceX vendor code in 2018 (SpaceNews confirmed). California Metal & Supply (acquired 2016) is a listed SpaceX supplier. Also secured $1.3M NASA/Boeing SLS contract. Company expanding US space business with new capital.
**Chokepoint assessment:** MODERATE. Specialty metals for rockets have qualification barriers, but SpaceX has multiple alloy suppliers (ATI, CRS, SeAH Besteel, Sphere Corp, HVM). Kencoa is one of several.
**Key risk:** Small-cap KOSDAQ; SpaceX is one of many customers; California Metal is a metals distributor, not a manufacturer with proprietary alloys.
**Investment thesis:** HOLD/DD. Confirmed SpaceX vendor since 2018. Worth monitoring but commodity metals distribution limits chokepoint power.
**Ticker:** 274090.KQ | Price: ~KRW 21,050

## TIER 2: Probable/Speculated SpaceX Relationship -- Worth Monitoring

### 7717.TWO -- Lightel Corporation (萊德光電-KY)
**What they supply:** Fiber optic communication components, satellite laser communication equipment, fiber lasers.
**Evidence:** Company explicitly lists "satellite laser communication equipment" in product portfolio. Taiwan analyst reports project satellite revenue reaching 60-70% of total revenue by 2026. Stock price tripled after Nov 2025 IPO (77 TWD to ~230+ TWD). Revenue +24.5% YoY in 2025.
**Chokepoint assessment:** SPECULATED. SpaceX builds laser inter-satellite links (ISLs) entirely in-house -- this is well-documented. If Lightel supplies components TO SpaceX's ISL production (fiber optic sub-components, test equipment, or optical elements), that's a different story. But no confirmed SpaceX contract found.
**Key risk:** SpaceX ISLs are in-house. If Lightel's satellite revenue is from non-SpaceX customers (Telesat, other constellation operators), the SpaceX narrative evaporates. Stock already 3x'd from IPO.
**Investment thesis:** HOLD/DD. If satellite laser comm components are genuinely for SpaceX ISL supply chain, this is interesting. But laser ISLs are SpaceX's most vertically integrated product. Need IR confirmation.
**Ticker:** 7717.TWO

## Key Observations

1. **Korea is emerging as a significant SpaceX supply chain hub**: Beyond the already-covered SeAH Besteel and Sphere Corp, at least 3 more Korean KOSDAQ companies (Kencoa, Sensorview, HVM) have confirmed or near-confirmed SpaceX relationships in specialty metals and radar antennas.

2. **Samsung Exynos NTN modem is the biggest development-stage story**: If Samsung wins the D2D modem for Starlink, this is a massive design win. But Qualcomm's X105 has a confirmed 12-18 month lead.

3. **Taiwan supply chain is mature -- few new confirmed names**: The 24 already-covered Taiwan tickers capture the core supply chain. New names (Unitech PCB, Lightel, MTI) are secondary/speculated.

4. **Japan remains a blind spot**: No confirmed Japanese SpaceX component suppliers found beyond already-covered Toray (3402.T) and Renesas (6723.T). Japanese passive component giants (Murata, TDK) likely have components in Starlink BOM but at commodity level.

5. **India is distribution, not supply chain**: Airtel and Jio are Starlink distributors. No Indian manufacturers confirmed in SpaceX hardware supply chain.

6. **Vietnam/Thailand manufacturing is Taiwan companies relocating**: The SE Asia manufacturing shift is existing Taiwan suppliers (WNC, Chin-Poon, UMT, Shenmao) moving factories, not new local companies entering the supply chain.

7. **SpaceX's Bastrop in-housing continues to compress supplier opportunity**: PCB factory operational, FOPLP chip packaging starting Q3 2026. This is a persistent headwind for all Taiwan component suppliers.
