## What's Being IPO'd
Full SpaceX (NOT Starlink spinoff):
- **Starlink**: ~79% of projected 2026 revenue (~$18.7B of ~$23.7B). 9M subscribers
- **Launch services**: Falcon 9, Starship
- **xAI** (merged Feb 2026 at ~$250B valuation): "Orbital data centers"
- **Defense/gov contracts**: Billions committed

At $1.75T / ~$23.7B rev = **74x 2026 revenue**. Very little margin for error.

## Is the IPO Priced Into Supply Chain Stocks?

**Partially, but not fully.**

1. **The March 25-26 rally was broad, not surgical** — 10-20% pops across ALL space stocks. Direct suppliers may still re-rate when S-1 names them specifically
2. **SpaceX discloses almost nothing today** — S-1 will be the first hard data on supplier relationships, cost structure, capex plans. This is a genuine information catalyst
3. **90% vertical integration limits supplier upside** — only niche specialists that SpaceX chose NOT to internalize matter
4. **xAI merger changes the calculus** — if market assigns most premium to AI, "space supplier" narrative could decouple

## Historical Analogs

- **ARM IPO (Sept 2023)**: +25% day 1, hit +51% by Dec, then stalled. Ecosystem halo lasted ~2-3 months
- **Tesla IPO (June 2010)**: Supplier stocks didn't re-rate at IPO. Real gains came years later as production scaled beyond in-house capacity. **Better analog for SpaceX**
- **Saudi Aramco (Dec 2019)**: Oil services saw brief sympathy rally, no durable supplier re-rating

## THE PLAYBOOK

### Phase 1: NOW → S-1 Filing (March-April)
**HOLD existing positions. Trim anything up 30%+ on pure IPO speculation.**
- Filing rally is priced in. Next catalyst is S-1 content, not the filing itself
- Risk of "buy the rumor, sell the filing" dip

### Phase 2: S-1 Publication (May-June)
**READ THE S-1. ADD selectively based on disclosures.**
- Mine: "Risk Factors" (named suppliers), "Cost of Revenue" (margins), "Properties" (in-house manufacturing = vertical integration map)
- If specific suppliers named → those stocks re-rate immediately
- If S-1 signals deeper in-housing → trim exposed names

### Phase 3: IPO Week + 30 Days (June-July)
**SELL into strength on any stock +40% from pre-filing levels.**
- ARM analog: halo peaks within 30 days of listing
- S&P 500 inclusion benefits SpaceX directly, not its suppliers
- Lock-up expiry ~Dec 2026 creates second volatility event

### Phase 4: First Quarterly Earnings (Q3 2026+)
**RE-ENTER on dips for long-term holds.**
- First-ever public quarterly will reveal segments, capex, subscriber metrics
- Market moves from "narrative" to "fundamentals"
- This is when real supplier alpha emerges

## Risk Management
- **No single SpaceX supplier >5% of portfolio** — 90% vertical integration means any can be disintermediated
- **Correlation risk**: All space stocks highly correlated on IPO narrative. Consider hedging via puts on ARKX or UFO ETFs
- **IPO delay**: If macro deteriorates, June slips to Q3/Q4. Expect 10-15% giveback across space names
- **Musk political risk**: DOGE involvement + political controversies could complicate IPO or dampen retail enthusiasm

## Bottom Line
The IPO filing rally has already happened. **The real alpha is in the S-1 disclosures** — SpaceX has been a black box and the first public filing will reveal supplier relationships, cost structures, and vertical integration plans. Long-term, scaling is the catalyst (Starship + 25M+ subscribers by 2028), not the IPO event itself.
