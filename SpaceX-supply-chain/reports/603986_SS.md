# 603986.SS - GigaDevice Semiconductor (Beijing)

## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 15/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Commodity / Indirect

### Thesis
GigaDevice's GD25Q128B 128Mbit SPI NOR flash is confirmed via teardown in the original Gen1 Starlink router, storing the bootloader and u-boot firmware. This is a real, hardware-verified design win. However, SPI NOR flash is the definition of a commodity semiconductor -- GigaDevice competes with Winbond, Macronix, Micron, and others on price alone. The part costs <$1. Gen2+ routers may have changed suppliers. GigaDevice is Shanghai-listed (603986.SS), adding geopolitical risk for Western investors, and trades at ~CNY 285 (~$31.8B mkt cap) with rich Chinese semiconductor valuations.

### SpaceX Relationship: CONFIRMED (Teardown-Verified, Gen1 Router Only)
- GD25Q128B SPI NOR flash confirmed in Gen1 Starlink router teardown (Oleg Kutkov, Applied Carbon)
- Stores primary bootloader (PBL), secondary bootloader (SBL/u-boot), and boot environment
- Commodity flash component; sub-$1 ASP
- Gen2/Gen3 routers may use different flash supplier -- no teardown confirmation
- **Estimate: SpaceX revenue <$5M total across all units sold, <0.05% of GigaDevice revenue**

### Value Chain Position
GigaDevice is a FABLESS semiconductor company designing memory and MCUs.
- **NOR Flash**: >70% of revenue; #2 global market share behind Winbond
- **MCU**: Growing segment; GD32 series competing with STM32
- **Sensors**: Smaller segment

GigaDevice is a fine NOR flash / MCU company recovering from a semiconductor downcycle, but the Starlink connection is trivial. Chinese listing adds a layer of geopolitical risk that makes this unsuitable for a SpaceX-themed portfolio.
