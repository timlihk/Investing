# HWM -- Howmet Aerospace

**Score: 40/100 | Chokepoint: 12/25 | HOLD**

## Thesis

Speculated SpaceX supplier as the world's #1 aerospace fastener manufacturer and a leading superalloy castings provider. Any rocket program needs high-performance fasteners and turbine-grade castings, and Howmet is the dominant supplier to Boeing, Airbus, Pratt & Whitney, GE Aerospace, and virtually every Western aerospace OEM. However, no direct SpaceX supplier relationship has been confirmed in any filing, IR communication, or teardown. SpaceX's vertical integration culture and Raptor's 3D-printed architecture may reduce traditional fastener/casting needs.

## SpaceX Relationship

SPECULATED -- No confirmed relationship. Howmet is the #1 global aerospace fastener supplier (Huck, Hi-Lok, LGP brands) and a top superalloy investment caster (single-crystal turbine blades, structural castings). It would be unusual for any rocket program to NOT use Howmet fasteners somewhere. But SpaceX's vertical integration and additive manufacturing approach may bypass traditional casting/fastener supply chains. SpaceX 3D-prints most Raptor engine components in-house.

## Verdict

**HOLD** -- on own merits as a best-in-class aerospace supplier with expanding margins and a commercial aviation upcycle tailwind. The operational improvement story is genuine and impressive. However, **AVOID** as a SpaceX play. The relationship is unconfirmed, rocket volumes are immaterial vs commercial aviation, and SpaceX's 3D-printing culture works against traditional casting/fastener suppliers. At 52x P/E, the stock is priced for the aero upcycle, not for SpaceX optionality.

**One-liner:** World's #1 aerospace fastener/casting house, but zero confirmed SpaceX relationship, 3D-printing headwind, and rocket volumes are a rounding error on $8.3B of commercial aero revenue.
