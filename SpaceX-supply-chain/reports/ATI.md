# ATI - ATI Inc (formerly Allegheny Technologies)
## Date: 2026-03-28
## Score: 72/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Duopoly Chokepoint

### Thesis
ATI is the preeminent US producer of aerospace-grade nickel superalloys and titanium mill products, holding sole-source positions on 6 of 7 critical jet engine alloys and serving as the only isothermal forger of engine disks for all three major OEMs. ATI almost certainly supplies materials that end up in rocket engines (including SpaceX's), but the relationship is structural and indirect — ATI is a chokepoint for the entire Western aerospace alloy supply chain, not a SpaceX pure-play. Stock has 4x'd in 52 weeks.

### SpaceX Relationship: SPECULATED (High Probability Indirect)
- No confirmed direct SpaceX supply contract in any filing or press release
- ATI's website lists "launch vehicles" among forged products applications
- SpaceX Raptor uses Inconel-family superalloys (proprietary SX300/SX500 variants) — SpaceX develops alloys in-house but requires feedstock superalloy inputs
- ATI is one of only ~3 Western producers of aerospace-grade nickel superalloy feedstock
- **Estimate: SpaceX-specific revenue likely <1% of ATI total**

### Value Chain Position
ATI sits at MIDSTREAM — melting, forging, rolling of specialty alloys. Converts raw metal inputs into aerospace-qualified alloy forms.
- **HPMC**: ~$600M/quarter revenue, ~24% EBITDA margin. Forgings, precision castings, powder metals, isothermal forging
- **AA&S**: ~$520M/quarter revenue, ~18.5% EBITDA margin. Flat-rolled products, long products (plate, sheet, bar, billet)

### Upstream/Downstream Map
```
UPSTREAM: Ti sponge (Japan, Kazakhstan) / Nickel (LME) / Cr, Co, Mo / Scrap
    ↓
ATI: HPMC (forgings, isothermal) + AA&S (flat-roll, long products)
    ↓
DOWNSTREAM: GE Aerospace, PW, Rolls-Royce (39% rev) / Boeing, Airbus, Lockheed
            SpaceX, Blue Origin, ULA (small) / Energy, MRO (~50% of engine sales)
```

### Moat Analysis
1. **Qualification moat (WIDE)**: 5-10yr cycles, already qualified on all major engine programs
2. **Sole-source positions**: Only isothermal forger for all 3 OEMs. 6/7 critical alloys
3. **Vertical integration**: Melts own superalloys AND forges — one of 2 US companies with this
4. **MRO flywheel**: 50% of engine sales now aftermarket/recurring
5. **Weakness**: No longer produces own Ti sponge (closed 2016) — dependent on imports

### Catalysts
- SpaceX IPO narrative bid, aerospace production ramp, A&D mix above 70%
- $1B EBITDA milestone in 2026, defense budget growth (F-35, NGAD, hypersonics)
- Titanium supply crunch (Russia sanctions, China concentration)

### Risks
- **Valuation stretched**: Stock 4x'd in 52 weeks. ~24x FY2026E EBITDA — high for materials
- Boeing/Airbus production delays, Ti sponge import dependency
- Customer concentration (3 engine OEMs, 2 airframe OEMs)
- SpaceX develops own alloys (SX300/SX500) and in-houses aggressively

### Verdict: HOLD
Genuinely excellent business — arguably the most critical specialty metals company in Western aerospace. Chokepoint score 20/25 is real and durable. But SpaceX relationship is indirect (<1% of revenue), and stock has already re-rated enormously at ~$151. Would be more attractive 30-40% lower as a broad aerospace chokepoint play.
