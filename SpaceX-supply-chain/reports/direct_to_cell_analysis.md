# Starlink Direct-to-Cell (D2C) Supply Chain Analysis
## Date: 2026-03-29

## THE BIG PICTURE: D2C Could Be Bigger Than Traditional Starlink

| Market | TAM | Timeline |
|--------|-----|----------|
| Traditional Starlink broadband | ~$41B | Current |
| D2C service revenue | $12-18B | By 2030-31 |
| Wireless market (5-10% capture) | **$55-110B** | Long-term |
| ARK/SpaceX total revenue potential | $100-200B | By 2027+ |

SpaceX paid **$19.6B for EchoStar spectrum** (AWS-4, H-Block, AWS-3) — the largest spectrum deal ever. This signals massive confidence in D2C economics.

## D2C Satellite Hardware — What's Different

- Phased arrays are **5x larger** than Gen1 → 4x bandwidth per beam, 20x link performance improvement
- **Custom SpaceX-designed silicon** for cellular-band beam-forming
- Next-gen V2 sats support **thousands of spatial beams** (100x throughput vs Gen1)
- V3 architecture (~2027): >1 Tbps fronthaul per satellite, 2,400 sq ft arrays
- **Key implication for STM**: 5x larger arrays = ~2,500-4,000+ antenna elements per D2C sat (vs ~500-800 standard). Chip demand scales proportionally

## Supply Chain Impact Rankings

### #1: STMicroelectronics (STM) — HIGHEST IMPACT
- Already confirmed at 5B+ chips, targeting 10B by 2027
- D2C satellites with 5x larger arrays = **3-5x more chips per satellite**
- 15,000 planned VLEO D2C satellites × thousands of chips each
- 10B by 2027 is the **floor, not the ceiling** with full V3 deployment
- **This is the single most important D2C supply chain fact**

### #2: Qualcomm (QCOM) — MAJOR NEW DESIGN WIN (Handset Side)
- **X105 5G Modem-RF** (MWC 2026): First Release 19 modem with native NR-NTN satellite connectivity
- Supports voice, data, video over satellite — not just emergency SOS
- 14.8 Gbps down / 4.2 Gbps up (terrestrial), satellite as integrated feature
- First devices late 2026, mass production 2027
- **Every future Snapdragon flagship phone gets satellite capability**
- TAM: ~400M+ Qualcomm chipsets/year × $2-5 NTN ASP uplift = **$0.8-2B incremental revenue**
- NR-NTN becomes table stakes — MediaTek and Samsung also developing competing modems

### #3: AMD (Versal) — More Processing Per Satellite
- Thousands of beams per D2C sat (vs fewer before) = more FPGA processing
- V3 sats with 1 Tbps throughput need more powerful Versal SoCs
- Incremental but confirmed

### #4: MediaTek — Competing NR-NTN Modem
- MT6825: First NTN-certified chipset on Skylo's network
- Targeting mid-range phones (vs Qualcomm's premium)
- Important for global D2C adoption but lower ASP

## What D2C Does NOT Create
- **No new ground infrastructure suppliers needed** — carriers' existing core networks handle D2C traffic. The satellite IS the cell tower
- **AST SpaceMobile (ASTS) is 95% vertically integrated** — limited external supply chain plays
- **MPWR**: No confirmed direct Starlink connection found in D2C research

## Competitive Landscape

| | Starlink D2C | AST SpaceMobile |
|---|---|---|
| Sats deployed | **650+** | 5 |
| Approach | Many smaller sats | Few massive sats |
| Bandwidth/sat | Lower (improving) | Higher (~100x per sat) |
| Carrier partners | T-Mobile, own Starlink Mobile brand | AT&T, Verizon, Vodafone, 40+ |
| Vertical integration | High (builds sats in-house) | ~95% |
| Spectrum | $19.6B EchoStar deal (50 MHz mid-band) | Carrier-provided |
| Timeline to US coverage | 2026 (already live, limited) | End of 2026 (targeted) |

Starlink's satellite count advantage (650 vs 5) and operational carrier partnerships give it a structural lead.

## Investment Implications

1. **STM thesis strengthened significantly** — D2C is a multiplier on chip demand, not just incremental. The RFOC segment (23.4% OM) is the crown jewel and D2C makes it bigger
2. **QCOM deserves a deeper look** — X105 NR-NTN is a structural shift. Not a SpaceX play per se, but Starlink is the catalyst that makes satellite connectivity standard in every phone
3. **D2C TAM ($12-18B by 2030) validates Starlink's growth trajectory** — which validates every confirmed supplier's volume assumptions
4. **The EchoStar spectrum deal ($19.6B) is the strongest signal** — SpaceX doesn't spend $20B on spectrum unless D2C is multi-tens-of-billions in revenue potential
