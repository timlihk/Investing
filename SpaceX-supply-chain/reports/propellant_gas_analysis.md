# Propellant & Industrial Gas Supply Chain Analysis
## Date: 2026-03-28

## Key Finding: HELIUM IS AN ACTIVE CHOKEPOINT CRISIS

### Helium — HIGH RISK (Active Crisis)
- Iran war triggered shutdown of Qatar's Ras Laffan complex → **~1/3 of global helium supply offline**
- QatarEnergy declared force majeure on LNG shipments (March 2026)
- Spot helium prices surged **40-100%** in weeks
- US federal helium reserve ended sales in 2025 → another supply hit
- **No synthetic substitute for helium** — it's extracted from natural gas wells
- SpaceX uses helium for: tank pressurization, engine purging, leak detection
- Falcon Heavy uses ~300,000 cubic meters per launch
- Starship's autogenous pressurization REDUCES but does NOT eliminate helium need

**Key supplier: Air Products (APD)** — world's largest helium supplier. JPMorgan upgraded to Overweight (March 2026) on helium pricing. Built helium plant for RasGas Qatar. NASA contracts >$140M. **This changes our prior AVOID thesis on APD** — the helium crisis makes APD interesting as a near-term tactical play even without direct SpaceX exposure.

### Liquid Methane — MEDIUM→LOW (Being Solved)
- Starship uses ~1,278 tonnes LCH4 per launch
- Previously required 200+ tanker trucks per launch (unsustainable)
- Linde $100M Brownsville ASU coming Q1 2026
- SpaceX building own ASU + LNG liquefaction plants at Starbase
- Texas natural gas abundant → feedstock not constrained
- **Bottleneck is liquefaction capacity, not methane availability**

### Krypton/Argon — LOW (Problem Engineered Away)
- **Critical discovery**: SpaceX switched from krypton to ARGON for V2 Mini Hall thrusters
- Elon Musk: "The transition to argon was tricky, but necessary, as krypton is too rare"
- Argon is ~100x cheaper than krypton, commodity byproduct of air separation
- V3 satellites feature next-gen argon Hall thruster (2.4x thrust, 1.5x Isp)
- **Not investable** — argon is a commodity

### RP-1 Kerosene — LOW (Declining Relevance)
- Falcon 9 uses ~127 tonnes per launch
- Produced by Haltermann Solutions, Monument Chemical, IPI — **all private**
- Declining as Starship (methane) replaces Falcon 9
- Not investable

## Investment Implications
1. **APD thesis has changed** — helium crisis makes APD tactically interesting despite no direct SpaceX relationship. World's largest helium supplier + JPMorgan upgrade + 40-100% price surge
2. **Linde (LIN)** benefits from both methane (Brownsville) AND helium distribution
3. **SpaceX is aggressively de-risking** — on-site ASU, methane liquefaction, krypton→argon switch. They clearly view propellant supply as strategic vulnerability
