# ETN - Eaton Corporation

## Date: 2026-03-29
## Score: 25/100
## Chokepoint Tier: Competitive with Differentiation

### Thesis
Eaton is a $138B market cap industrial conglomerate with a well-established aerospace segment (~$4.4B annual revenue, ~25% operating margin) that makes propulsion tanks, valves, regulators, and feed systems for space and launch applications. Eaton developed the first metal-to-metal seal pyrovalve and has heritage on launch vehicles, satellites, and deep space missions. However, SpaceX's primary valve supplier is Marotta Controls (30,000+ CoRe valves delivered), and Eaton's space revenue is a tiny fraction of its $27.4B total. The stock at $357 prices in AI/data center power management, not rockets.

### SpaceX Relationship: SPECULATED (Low-Moderate Probability)
- Eaton's website lists "space and launch" as an aerospace market with propulsion valves and tanks
- No confirmed SpaceX contract in any filing or earnings call
- Marotta Controls is the confirmed primary SpaceX valve supplier (30,000 CoRe valves)
- Moog also competes for space propulsion valves
- **Estimate: SpaceX-specific revenue likely <0.1% of Eaton total ($27.4B), possibly zero**

### Value Chain Position
Eaton sits at TIER-1 COMPONENT level -- manufacturing valves, tanks, and fluid management systems.
- **Electrical Americas**: $12.8B (47% of rev), 29.8% operating margin -- the star segment (data centers)
- **Electrical Global**: $6.3B (23%)
- **Aerospace**: ~$4.4B (16%), 24-26% operating margin -- includes space/launch
- **Vehicle / eMobility**: $3.9B (14%) -- being spun off

### Verdict: AVOID
Eaton is an excellent industrial compounder -- buy it for AI data center power management, not rockets. Space and launch is a marketing bullet point on a $27.4B revenue behemoth. Even if Eaton supplies some legacy pyrovalves to SpaceX, the revenue would be invisible at this scale.
