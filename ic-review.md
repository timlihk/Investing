## Top-Level Committee Read

- The highest-quality underwriting cases in the repo are mostly true bottlenecks or toll collectors, not the most promotional end-market names.
- `memory-supercycle` is strongest where the thesis is complexity monetization rather than generic memory beta.
- `ai-photonics` is strongest where the company controls an enabling layer with real pricing power, and weakest where the optical angle is too diluted, too early, or too cyclical.
- CHIPS-linked support is helpful where it exists, but it is not a substitute for moat, margin quality, or valuation discipline.

## Notes

- The project memos are deliberately skeptical. They are meant to challenge the existing repo thesis, not simply summarize it.
- China-listed names can be excellent businesses and still receive lower committee sizing because access and geopolitics can dominate fundamentals.
- Several names in both projects remain investable only as satellite or tactical positions because the bottleneck logic is plausible but not yet fully proven in the P&L.
